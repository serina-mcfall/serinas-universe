## Weapons — Wǔ Yǐng Zhū (乌影珠) — "Dark Shadow Beads"

**Inspired by:** Wu Shi Guang's black prayer beads in Veil of Shadows.

**The strand:** 27 black lacquer beads on a silken cord, worn on his LEFT wrist. Looks like a monk's meditation strand — a scholar's cultural affectation. It is not a cultural affectation. It is his leash AND his arsenal.

**How they work — CLOSED LOOP:**
- Every time he conjures darkness (blade, cloak, spell), his shadow tries to grow. The beads CATCH the excess and cache it. Bead by bead, stored as shadow.
- When he needs a weapon, **he reaches for his wrist instead of the void between stars.** One bead opens softly (lacquer cracks like an egg), the collapsed star inside flows into his hand as black liquid-light, and takes the form he calls. Spent beads are hollow and dull, waiting to be refilled.
- **This means he does NOT have to reach into fresh void darkness for every fight.** He spends cached shadow. His shadow does not keep growing. He has turned what was supposed to destroy him into a closed loop.
- **Empty beads = dead lacquer. Full beads = internal depth like a tiny collapsed star inside.** The fuller the strand, the heavier it feels on his wrist. He gauges shadow-inventory by weight.

**The Black Arsenal (forms the beads can take):**
1. **Threads** (1 bead each) — thin black filament, capture/bind, cuts the light around itself.
2. **Net** (5-7 beads) — woven threads, wide enough to cover a small room, absorbs magic cast inside, holds or tightens at his will. The scholar's mercy weapon.
3. **Daggers** (1-2 beads) — everyday silent blade. Cuts physical/magical/spiritual.
4. **Swords** (3-5 beads) — performance weapon. For when he wants to be SEEN as a swordsman.
5. **The Cloak — "The Night He Wears"** (2-3 beads) — cloak of shadow, hood hides face/hair/the mark. Cuts light around it: eyes slide off, minds refuse to focus. How the Shadow Prince actually moves through his province unseen. **Aria can see through it — her forgetting-fog-resistance also resists the "don't notice me" effect. First time she does, he is STARTLED, which is not a thing he has ever been.**
6. **The Spear That Pierces Light** (~half the strand) — pierces not just flesh but LIGHT ITSELF. Cuts through barriers, holy shields, illusions of brightness, Solenthera priest wards. **"The weapon that denies the lie of holiness."** Thematically perfect for a Shi Sect son refusing his mother's lie of necessary darkness. He will not reach for it unless something serious is happening.
7. **Arrows That Pierce Anything** (2-3 beads) — points of shadow carrying intent. Cut through armour, ward, wall, ghost. His WANTING is the aiming system.

## The Three Words (one-syllable spells — his signature)

Each must be spoken OUT LOUD. Each is a declaration. Each reveals what Yuè'àn is willing to do for the people he loves. They are economies of mercy — the scholar's answers to situations where violence would be easier.

**回 — HUÍ — "RETURN" — The Word for Aria**
When Aria is in danger, he speaks this word and she is DRAGGED across space and arrives at his side. Not teleported — pulled. Cannot harm her; his intent cannot hurt her. **The word is a confession he can't take back.** He could have chosen *shield* or *save* — he chose a word that means BELONGS TO. Aria belongs at his side. His body decided this before his mind admitted it. Every use of huí is him declaring, to the world and to himself, that she is where she should be. Aria will figure this out after the second or third time. She will not say anything. But she will know.
- Cost: 3-5 beads. Register: Wu Huo measured voice. Sounds like an ordinary sentence until the world obeys.

**忘 — WÀNG — "FORGET" — The Word for Mercy**
Erases a specific memory from a witness. Not the whole mind — just the fragment his intent points at. Used when the alternative is killing the witness. **The same tool the ruling class of Aurum uses. - **His rules:** Never on a child. Never on someone who has earned the truth. Never — NEVER — on Aria.
- **The day he realises he couldn't use it on her EVEN IF HE TRIED** (her gift makes her unforgetable, the spell bounces off her) is the day he understands Aria is outside his tools entirely. She will always know what he truly is. And she chose him anyway.
- Cost: 2-4 beads. Register: Wu Huo pitched EVEN LOWER — almost a sigh. The reluctance is part of the spell. Cast with grief, carries less cruelty than Aurum's fog.

**执 — ZHÍ — "HOLD" — The Most Powerful Word**
Whatever he points at HOLDS. A wound holds shut. A door holds against an army. A dying heart keeps beating. A falling body holds midair. Sustained command — reality pushes back and the beads drain one per heartbeat.
- **The scene where it matters:** Someone beloved is dying. Gēnmù or Aria. No healer close enough. Yuè'àn looks at the dying one, speaks 执, and the damage HOLDS. The Fellowship has to save them before his beads run out. He will spend every bead, and when the beads are empty he will keep speaking the word with his own raw shadow, and he will keep speaking it until his body is empty too.
- Cost: 10+ beads for short holds; can empty the whole strand for longer ones. Register: FULL Zhao Yuan Zhou spell-voice. The one time he does not ration. Air distorts around his throat, syllable IS law, candles lean, even his own shadow goes still.

The fragment of Yuèlì inside him goes quiet around the earth twin's grandson. Gēnmù does not know any of this. He just sits there sometimes humming and Yuè'àn's internal war goes to sleep.

I have been doing this my whole life. I did not become that. I am still here."** Each object is proof of a refusal he made before he knew there was a game.
- **The box is his memory AGAINST his own forgetting.** The theme of the universe is carved into his most private ritual. He keeps small things that remember who he was trying to become, for the days he forgets.

Both would mean nothing to a stranger. **Aria and Yuè'àn have each been keeping a hidden proof-of-selfhood their entire lives, and neither of them knows the other has one.**
