## Isenholt Overview

- Scandinavian + Mongolian blend. The frozen watch. Guard the mountain passes.
- Province of Caerndrath but culturally distinct. Shares mountain border with Aoli.
- People are born in winter, live in winter, die in winter. Bred to guard and hold the line.
- Organized into TRIBES/CLANS (not a single kingdom). A Khan rules all but travels with the people.
- Architecture: Yurts (mobile) + longhouses (permanent) + turf houses (old ways). Blended.
- Landscape: Fjordlands, Mongolian steppes, deep mountain passes, snow-capped peaks.

## Beast Bonding Rite

- Age: 12 years old (rite of passage)
- Only MALES go through this
- The young warrior is taken to the mountains by the clan elder, told to listen to the mountains
- Left alone for a WEEK with only clothes, a bow, and arrows
- The bond is an instant spark -- like finding the missing half. Senses open. Eyes change colour based on the animal.
- The animal doesn't just become a companion -- it becomes an AMPLIFIER of senses
- The clan doesn't CHOOSE the boy -- the mountain and the beast choose

## The Warrior's Journey (Hierarchy)

Six words that map a boy's entire life:
1. **Unbonded** -- before the rite
2. **Bonded** -- after the beast bond
3. **Warrior** -- proven in combat
4. **Stonebred** -- clan chieftain (won through the Choosing)
5. **Stoneborn** -- leader of ALL clans (won through the Choosing combat)
6. **Greysworn** -- retired elder, sworn to the Greyhearth council

## The Choosing (Leadership Selection)

- Each chieftain sends their most fit warrior champion
- FREE FOR ALL -- no weapons, just fists and beast
- NOT first to kill -- warriors are sacred, the tribe needs them all
- Last one standing becomes Stoneborn; the other champions become Stonebred (chieftains) of their clans
- Previous chieftains become the council of elders

## God-Touched Women

Women do NOT go into the mountains for bonding. They carry the OLD magic -- deeper, direct connection to the land (not through an animal intermediary).

Only a chosen few women are "God-touched":
- **Silvermyst** (Healers) -- silver eyes, almost blind-looking. Flower: white ivy (God's Tears). Connected to Yanlong's grief.
- **Skyborn** (Listeners) -- darkest blue eyes, almost like night. Animal: owl. Hear whispers in the wind, through the trees.
- **Rootweavers** (Growers) -- green eyes that glow. Symbol: Hearthroot. Can grow anything. Their power threads through earth like weaving.

### The God-Touch Ritual
- When a girl is born, the Hearthkeeper (clan mother) takes the baby to the Living Cave
- The Living Cave: light from above, stone pillar in the centre (not a tree), a river (not a lake) flowing around it, stepping stones to a central island
- The pillar has words no longer readable in a language long lost
- The Hearthkeeper mixes water from the underground source with dirt from the earth (symbolically: Yunsheng + Yanlong) and draws a line on the baby's brow
- The baby's eyes open and slowly change colour: silver (healer), dark blue (listener), green (grower), or nothing (normal)
- If god-touched: celebration and joy but also FEAR. She must be hidden. Caerndrath kills commoners with magic.

### The Veil Tradition
- ALL women in Isenholt wear a ceremonial headdress that covers their eyes at special occasions
- Not just the god-touched -- ALL women. This way the god-touched can hide in plain sight.
- The tradition is so old no one questions it. It's a way of hiding without hiding.

### The Hearthkeeper
- The clan mother. She performs marriage tattoos with magic. Keeps the fire, keeps the home, keeps the clan alive.
- The Stoneborn's Hearthkeeper sits on the Greyhearth council and speaks for ALL women of ALL clans.

### The Volugan (Maiden Priestess)
- The historian, the archivist for the clans. Keeps all records.
- Not allowed to marry or have children. Their life becomes the scrolls.
- A woman offered the Volugan role instead of an ivyless marriage can accept this path.

## The Greyhearth Council
- Council of retired elders (Greysworn) from all six clans
- Plus the Stoneborn's Hearthkeeper (one woman, speaks for all women)
- Advises the Stoneborn. Holds wisdom earned in blood and winters.

## Marriage Ceremony -- The Walk Between Fires

### God's Tears (White Ivy)
- White ivy that grows only in the highest snow. NOT green but purest white.
- Yanlong weeps in his sleep, dreaming of Yunsheng. His tears become the ivy.
- Before a wedding, the bride, her sisters, mother, and grandmother climb the mountain to pick the ivy.
- If no ivy is found: the wedding is considered unsanctioned by the gods. These marriages are shunned. Children of ivyless marriages are outcasts.
- **Temgar is the result of an ivyless marriage.** His mother was given the choice to become a Volugan but refused.

### The Ceremony
- Bride walks between two fires. Mother and grandmother on either side.
- They leave her just before the centre -- she crosses the centre ALONE.
- On the other side, her new mother and grandmother lead her to the groom.
- The groom leads her to the Hearthkeeper (clan mother).
- The Hearthkeeper gives the wedding tattoo on both wrists.

### The Wedding Tattoo
- An intricate design with no beginning or ending -- eternal knot.
- Reflects the husband's clan animal + the woman's identity (god-touched type or her favourite plant/flower).
- The Hearthkeeper passes a bit of magic into the bond as she weaves the tattoo.
- The bond does not break unless the tattoo is severed/destroyed.
- Only one debonding in legend: the woman died shortly after. The man disappeared.

### Outcasts
- Ivyless marriage couples live at the clan boundary with other outcasts.
- Their children cannot go into the mountain for bonding. They become butchers, blacksmiths, woodcutters.
- Temgar was never supposed to have Meila (his direwolf) at all.
