## About Serina

- Serina is a world-builder and storyteller building a multi-story fantasy universe
- She has dyslexia and AuDHD (reflected in her protagonist Aria as well)
- Her husband is Gavin, who helps with technical/dev questions
- She uses WSL (Windows Subsystem for Linux) with username `serin` on Windows
- Her GitHub repo is at `/home/serina/GitHub/Personal/serinas-universe`
- She works in the terminal by preference, not VS Code GUI
- She uses Claude.ai chat with a persona called "Wen" (Wenji) -- a nine-tailed fox character who is also a god in her world

## Creative Style

- She is a visual thinker -- uses mood boards, reference images (often AI-generated via ChatGPT/Midjourney), and aesthetic inspiration
- She builds world-first, then places characters into the world: "I like to build my world then stick the characters in this world and tell their story"
- She works in bursts of intense creative energy, often dreaming about her world overnight and coming back with new ideas
- She is a pattern thinker who pulls one thread and lights up the whole tapestry -- ideas cascade and connect naturally
- She names things by feel and sound -- she says names aloud and chooses what "sings" or "lands right"
- She draws heavily from Chinese/East Asian culture, Celtic/Norse/Germanic culture, Mediterranean/Roman/Byzantine culture, and Mongolian/Siberian/Inuit culture
- Her key inspirations: Lord of the Rings, Game of Thrones, Avatar: The Last Airbender, xianxia/cultivation fiction, C-dramas, steampunk

## Preferences

- Loves high fantasy, xianxia, history, ancient worlds, and steampunk -- all blended into one layered universe
- Prefers warm, parchment-toned UI for her world bible (not dark themes)
- Wants her world bible to be interactive (JSX/React artifact in Claude) with backup to GitHub
- Likes "short and sweet" names for everyday use (e.g., sects go by Yan, Wen, Yun, Fen, Hun, Shi rather than full ceremonial names)
- Values the emotional weight of her world -- tragedy, sacrifice, love, and moral complexity are central themes
