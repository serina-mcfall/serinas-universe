## Status
Deep character work on Aria Tyramare — building her interior life one question at a time before rewriting her Bible entry. The plan is to do all six Fellowship members this way, starting with Aria because everyone else's arc orbits hers.

## Question 1 — Interior Baseline (ANSWERED)

**How does Aria feel from the inside on an ordinary morning in Aurum?**

- **Restless but content** — content in her workshop, but a clock ticks at the back of her chest. Wants to go somewhere, doesn't know where, doesn't know how. The wanting without the map.
- **Not lonely** — she has the mechanical bird. Her best friend for half her life. She breathed life into him without knowing what she was doing. One real friend is a full cup for Aria — she doesn't measure friendship by quantity.
- **Not loud, not quiet — the middle voice.** Doesn't shout. Doesn't whisper. Just *keeps talking* until the room listens. Has a temper. Has a voice. Knows right from wrong and says so. Zhúhuǒ's spine without the conflict.
- **Brain always ticking** — new inventions, new thoughts, pattern recognition running in the background. AuDHD working FOR her.
- **Funny** — dry-funny, observational. The one-line that catches people off guard. Humour as an extension of pattern recognition — she sees the ridiculous thing before anyone else.
- **A foody and a snob about it** — walks three extra blocks for the vendor who does the thing right. Her palate is sharper than Aurum should have let it be. (Foreshadowing: Solis, where food is a religion, will feel like home before anyone explains why.)

## Question 2 — The Gremlin (ANSWERED)

**The gremlin is a shapeshifter. Three faces:**

1. **Most of the time** — effort-avoidance voice. *Don't walk three blocks. Just go to the nearer bakery. Your inventions will fail. Don't bother.* Aria shouts it down **out loud**: "GO BACK TO SLEEP." The whole neighbourhood knows the sound. Passersby outside her workshop say "oh, Aria is at it again." The gremlin is a local landmark.

2. **Sometimes** — the orphanage instructor who told her she wasn't enough. Slips in when she's tired or when an invention actually fails.

3. **Rarely, when she lets it slip in** — her parents' voices. "They didn't want you. They didn't love you. That's why they left you." **This is the only one she can't shout down** — she has no memory to counter it, no letter, no evidence. When this voice comes, she just goes very quiet. The bird notices and whispers *patience, soon.*

**The devastating truth Aria doesn't know:** The gremlin is lying hardest with this voice. Zhúhuǒ would burn the sky to get to her. Mínhuì watches her from across crowded markets and has been breaking silently for sixteen years. The parents she thinks abandoned her are the two people in the world who want her most.

**Love-hate relationship with it** — sometimes it's the kick she needs. Spite is its own fuel in the Aria economy. She's been using it for sixteen years.

**Self-awareness level:** She KNOWS at the level of thought that the gremlin is just a voice, not her. But at the level of *feeling*, when she's tired or hurting, it feels real. The reminder that "it's not me, it's just the voice" is its own kind of labour she has to do every time.

## Question 3 — Surface want vs. deeper need (ANSWERED)

**Aria's wanting is a bouquet — four surface wants braided around one unnameable pull:**

Surface wants (what she CAN name):
- **Curiosity** — engineer's hunger. Wants to see how things work, take them apart and put them back together. The whole world is a puzzle box she hasn't cracked yet.
- **Appetite** — wants to try real food, unregulated, street food, spices she's never smelled before. Her food-snobbery is a sensory hunger, not just a quirk.
- **Exploration** — the world is huge and she has seen less than 1% of it.
- **Roots** — she feels out of place in the sky cities even though she can't say why.

**The unnameable pull underneath:** Her divine earth-and-water bloodlines are trying to get home. Every step she takes in Aurum, some cellular part of her is saying "this isn't where you grew from — your people are down there." She experiences this as restlessness and "out of place," because she has no language for *missing a place you have never been.*

**Key insight:** The word ROOTS she used is not coincidence. Her unknown cousin is literally named Gēnmù ("root and tree"). Her unconscious mouth reaches for the exact word for the thing she doesn't know about herself.

**Story payoff:** The moment Aria first sets foot on real earth — real dirt, real stone, real grass — something in her will quietly unlock. Not dramatically. The way your body exhales before your brain notices it was holding its breath. One of the most important moments in the whole story, and it'll happen BEFORE any of the big plot beats.

## Question 4 — The Mechanical Bird (ANSWERED)

**Name:** **Xiǎo Niǎo (小鸟)** — Little Bird. **Niǎo Niǎo** when Aria is being tender.

**What he is to her:** Pet AND friend AND family. Er Shi Qi energy (the white cat from Ming Yi's story — silent, loyal, would end the world for his person). But deeper: **a piece of Aria's own spirit wearing metal.** Not all of her — a fragment. Enough that he woke up. Enough that he stayed. Some of her lives inside him; some of him lives inside her.

**How she made him:** She was **ten years old**, tinkering, trying to build a songbird she had only ever seen in a book — because songbirds don't exist in Aurum (they belong to the earth below). She was reaching for the earth through the only language her city would let her speak (mechanics). Ordinary scrap and brass and copper. She didn't know she was pouring her spirit into the metal. Her divine blood poured out through her hands without permission, because that's what happens when a spirit-fox god's daughter pours her whole small self into a picture from a book.

**The moment he woke:** She remembers it vividly. His eyes opened and they were NOT metal — they were **blue, shining, bright, full of life and love.** That colour came from HER — a piece of her spirit showing through the only window it had. She sighed, tickled his chin, and said **"Welcome, little bird."** Not "what?" Not "how?" Just *welcome*, because on a level below thinking she had been waiting for him.

**What this tells us about Aria:** She greets magic the way she greets everything else — directly, without drama, with her hands. She tickled the chin of an impossibility and welcomed it. That's how she'll greet the rest of the story when it comes for her.

**Mínhuì connection:** The day Aria accidentally imbued her spirit into the bird, Mínhuì (the spirit fox god hiding in Aurum, Aria's mother) felt the ripple across the veil. She KNEW in that instant her daughter had her gift. She couldn't tell her. She went home that night and wept harder than she had in a century. The bird is PROOF her daughter is who she is.

## Question 5 — The Orphanage + Phineas Thatcher (ANSWERED)

**The orphanage: NEUTRAL with teeth.** Not cruel, not kind — **stern.** Headmistress ran it efficiently. Aria was fed, clothed, educated. And she wouldn't stop asking "why this why that can't I why can't I" — so the teachers and headmistress labelled her TROUBLE and brushed her off.

**Why this is the worst possible childhood for Aria specifically:** She could have fought cruelty — she has a temper. You can't fight being brushed off. The ambient stern neutrality of an orphanage that labelled her "trouble" and then stopped listening IS her gremlin. The voice that says "your inventions will fail, don't bother, you're not enough" is the ambient temperature of that orphanage wearing her down one dismissal at a time.

### PHINEAS THATCHER

The ONE exception. An old tinker — an engineer — who taught Aria his craft. The only adult in her childhood who looked at her and saw not "trouble" but "a spark." He started her on her path of **artimancy** (the forbidden art — magic woven with mechanism, the same art that secretly powers Aurum's sky cities). He encouraged her dreams even when the rest of the orphanage called them blasphemy. He was also the only other person who ever saw Xiǎo Niǎo as more than metal. He said "oh, little one, this is beautiful."

**He spoke out one too many times.** He said something he shouldn't have said. One night he disappeared.

**The forgetting fog closed over him.** Everyone in the orphanage forgot him within days — his name slipped out of every mouth as if it had never been said, his tools were sold, his workshop cleared, his memory wiped.

**Except Aria.** The fog doesn't work on her (divine blood). She is the only person in all of Aurum who remembers Phineas Thatcher existed. She says his full name — first AND last — sometimes in the workshop when she's working on something that reminds her of him. Not as a prayer. As a **receipt**. Keeping track. Refusing to let Aurum win.

**Aria's divine gift in Aurum is REMEMBERING.** Mínhuì's daughter, raised in a forgetting city, became a rememberer. Of course she did.

### The Growing Archive of the Disappeared

By sixteen, Aria has been watching people disappear for years. **Examples she holds in her head:**
- The girl in the flower shop who was there and then wasn't — Aria said "where's the girl who used to work here?" and got blank stares.
- The child who sold newspapers on the corner — gone four days in a row, the adults who bought papers from him every morning don't remember he existed.

She sees the pattern. She sees that every disappeared person had "something a little strange" about them. She does NOT yet have the word MAGIC or HARVESTING — the word is taboo in Aurum, her brain doesn't reach for it easily. But she knows in her GUT it is WRONG. She is BUILDING a theory she hasn't been given permission to call a theory.

**She doesn't yet know she is the next name on her own list.**
- The fog doesn't work on her
- She builds impossible things
- Her bird has blue shining eyes
- She asks the wrong questions
- The ruling class's blood-magic hunters would mark her instantly if they focused on her long enough

**Mínhuì is the reason they haven't.** The spirit fox god has been weaving the veil around her own daughter for sixteen years. Bending the fog subtly so adults' eyes slide off Aria. The reason Aria is "labelled difficult and then left alone" is that Mínhuì is KEEPING HER SAFE from across the market she's never allowed to cross. The orphanage felt neutral-stern to Aria but was the closest thing to safety Mínhuì could give her. Phineas Thatcher finding Aria may have been a Mínhuì nudge too — but the spirit fox god could not save Phineas when the hunters came for him. She could only save her daughter. One.

## Question 6 — What her brain feels like (ANSWERED)

**Serina's EXACT words — treat as verbatim:** "It's like being in a car going 100 miles an hour and has no brakes. It's like being in a room full of 100s of TVs all on at the same time telling different stories and not being able to pick them out. Like a mouse running forever in a wheel with no stop in sight. It's loud, it's chaotic, and it's not quiet."

**CRITICAL:** Serina confirmed this describes her own brain while writing/painting/coding. Aria's brain IS Serina's brain, loaned. When writing Aria's hyperfocus/burnout scenes, use real lived experience — not research.

**The reframe:** The workshop isn't quiet for Aria. The workshop is the only place LOUD enough to out-shout the ambient hundred TVs. Hyperfocus isn't flow — it's the work becoming the loudest TV in the room, and Aria riding that one signal through the noise. This is why she can't stop building. The work is a MERCY. The moment she puts the gearbox down, the hundred TVs come back to full volume and she is alone in the static.

**Dyslexia as architecture:** Words on a page are dead weight. Words don't move. Her brain is a roundabout at rush hour; pages are one-way streets. The blueprint she can't read becomes an engine she can rebuild blindfolded. Sketches are her native language (and accidental camouflage from inspectors).

**She draws her inventions.** Her sketches are a MESS — layers, detours, ricochets, tea stains she left on purpose. Nobody else can read them. She can read all of them instantly, years later, because reading them is remembering. Sometimes creatures appear in the corners (bird, nine-tailed fox, dragon) that she doesn't remember drawing — her blood drawing things her head has never been told.

**Burnout:** Serina named it plainly — **AuDHD burnout**. Not a crash, a STATE. The work she loves is right there and she cannot lift a wrench. The hundred TVs go to static. Hunger is weird. Brittle, thin-edged, muted. Gremlin gets LOUDER because she has no energy to shout it down. She has learned to ride it out — the fighting only makes it longer. Xiǎo Niǎo sits in it with her, still, present, blue eyes lit, sometimes whispering *patience, soon* — and during burnout those words land as COMPANY, not hope.

**Story hook:** The Fellowship will have to learn how to be with her when she burns out. The first one who figures out "don't cheer her up, sit with her, bring food she doesn't have to think about, keep Xiǎo Niǎo safe, don't rush her" earns a depth of her trust nobody else gets. (Yuè'àn guess — his darkness and her burnout speak the same language.)

## Question 7 — Does she know she's different (ANSWERED)

**Serina's words:** "She knows she is different. She wears it with pride. She does not give a damn what others think of her. She learned a long time ago that the only opinion that matters is her own."

**Reframe:** This is Aria's ARMOUR. Her self-possession is not arrogance — it is survival wearing a crown. When the world refused to validate her, she decided she would stop waiting. She made herself responsible for the job of deciding her own value.

**What this changes about the gremlin:** The gremlin's normal weapons — "everyone thinks you're weird, nobody likes you, you're strange" — bounce off. She already made peace with being weird years ago. The ONLY gremlin-weapon that still wounds her is the PARENT-VOICE, because the parents are the one judgment she never got to make unilaterally. The gremlin's cruelest weapon is also the one that is lying the hardest. When the truth eventually comes (Zhúhuǒ + Mínhuì + a long terrible silence), the last weapon is disarmed.

**Key insight:** Aria is NOT the prophecy child who needs saving. Aria has already won a battle most people never figure out. She has an identity. What she needs from the Fellowship is not rescue — it is **WITNESSES**.

**When attacked — THE MOM STARE:** Not hot, not cold. Serina's words: "You know that mom stare we all dread, the one that says 'no you did not and if you did you had better run and run quick and far cause I will cut you.'" She doesn't flare up. She stops, puts down whatever is in her hands, lands her eyes on the person flat and absolutely still. One short sentence at half her normal volume. Xiǎo Niǎo's blue eyes lock on the target too. Some people have left just because the bird was also judging them. **She inherited the Mom Stare from a mother she has never met** — from Mínhuì, the spirit fox god whose version of that look goes bone-deep. Some things ride on the blood.

## Question 8 — One tiny detail that's completely hers (ANSWERED — FOUR details, all doing work)

**1. The messy bun.** Held together with whatever was closest — a screwdriver, a pencil, sometimes a knife. NOT style. Inventory. Her workshop tools live in her hair when they aren't in her hands. (Companion detail: Xiǎo Niǎo sometimes takes tools OUT of her bun with his beak when he notices she's looking for them.)

**2. Cupcakes. Strawberry cake with vanilla frosting.** The most childish thing on any bakery shelf. Walks three extra blocks for the good ones. **She has been being her own parent since she was old enough to buy her own cupcakes, and the parent she chose to be for herself is a kind one.** Strawberry and vanilla are not Aurum flavours — they come from the earth below. Even her comfort food is a love letter to a place she has never been.

**3. She talks to herself out loud. Arguments with herself.** The whole neighbourhood hears her. Passersby outside the workshop laugh fondly and say **"oh, Aria's at it again."** This includes shouting "GO BACK TO SLEEP" at the gremlin. She's not lonely — she's EXTERNALISING her AuDHD cognition to track it. Two Arias in the workshop, one hand with the wrench, the other gesturing. **The neighbours have adopted her without her knowing** — old women nod at her, the cupcake vendor slips extras on tired days. Aria thinks she is alone. She is not.

**4. THE BOOK. Phineas Thatcher gave her a book.** When his workshop was being cleared out after he was disappeared, **ten-year-old Aria hid the book.** She has kept it hidden for six years. It is her MOST PRECIOUS POSSESSION. Contents TBD but strong candidates: forbidden artimancy guide, Phineas's personal journal (possibly including notes about Aria herself and her bird), or the children's book that contained the songbird illustration Xiǎo Niǎo was built from. She talks to the book. "Okay, Phineas, what do I do about this flange..." Then answers herself in the voice she remembers him having.
