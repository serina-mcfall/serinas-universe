AURUM — THE GILDED SKY CITIES\n\n═══ THE ONE-LINE TRUTH ═══\n\nAurum is a civilization that fled the ground to escape a corruption — and invited that same corruption back in centuries later to keep itself in the sky. It is gold and iron above the clouds, beautiful and built on horror, and Aria was born here.\n\n═══ AURUM THE GILDED ═══\n\nThe name Aurum means GOLD. The realm is sunset-coded — every island catches the long evening light, every pagoda roof gleams, every brass mechanism shines. Outsiders see a daydream of floating islands trailing waterfalls into clouds, airships and flying ship-homes drifting between them, golden-domed pavilions catching the sun. Insiders see the same daydream, and most of them never look down at what holds it up.\n\nThe subtitle of Aurum is *\"Gold and iron in the sky.\"* The gold is what the realm shows the world. The iron is the brass-and-steam machinery that keeps the islands aloft, the engineering laced through every island's underbelly. And beneath even that, the realm's deepest secret — the blood that actually feeds the levitation. Three layers. Each tier of Aurum society sees a different one.\n\n═══ THE SKY-CITY VISION ═══\n\nAurum is not one city — it is a constellation of floating islands held aloft above a continent the upper classes have learned not to think about. The islands are real land, with original geology, native ecosystems, native flora, native trees. They are pieces of the continent below, torn up by primordial cosmic-tier magic in the ancient past and lifted into the sky.\n\nEach island carries pagoda architecture fused with Victorian-steampunk engineering — pagoda forms with brass clockwork integrated into them, not stacked beside them. Engineered waterfalls pour from the islands' edges into the cloud layer below. Greenery thrives on the upper tiers because the islands brought their soil and their forests with them when they rose.\n\nMobile flying ship-homes drift between the static islands, carrying the noble class. Some are small minor-house vessels; some are great pagoda-bearing junk-style sky-vessels rivalling small cities. The grandest vessels belong to the Royal House. The nobles do not want to live among commoners; their flying ship-homes let them live near the static islands without ever touching the soil of one.\n\nThe AESTHETIC: Asian pagoda + Victorian steampunk FUSED. Most fantasy floating-cities choose one — Aurum is both at once. Pagoda atop steampunk. Gilded. Lush. Machined. The mood is sunset, always sunset — the gilded hour drawn out forever. Beautiful and built on horror.\n\n═══ THE TWO HALVES — ABOVE THE CLOUDS AND THE UNDERROCK BELOW ═══\n\nEvery floating island has TWO HALVES, and they are not equally seen.\n\n**ABOVE THE CLOUDS** — the visible Aurum. Lush native greenery, engineered waterfalls, pagoda neighbourhoods stacked along the upper tiers, merchant quarters, tradeworker districts, public plazas. Daylight. The Aurum the rest of the world believes is the whole story. This is where most outsiders ever go and where most Aurum-born sky-folk are born and die.\n\n**THE UNDERROCK BELOW** — the lowest caste, hidden inside the islands' rock-bodies. The carved stone underbellies where the engines roar, the bioluminescent reservoir glows, the goggled workers tend the machinery that holds the city above their heads. Dim and quiet. Beautiful in its own dark way. The folk who live here have a name for themselves the upper classes refuse to use. (See `aurum-underrock`.)\n\nThe upper class calls the lowest tier *the Bilge.* The people who live there call themselves *the Underrock folk.* Two names for the same place — and each time the names appear in dialogue, the saga teaches the reader who looks down on whom.\n\n═══ THE SIX-TIER CLASS SYSTEM — STATUS BY ALTITUDE ═══\n\nIn Aurum, class is LITERAL altitude. The higher you fly, the more you matter. Each tier sees a different version of the truth.\n\n| Tier | Class | Lives | Sees |\n|---|---|---|---|\n| 1 (lowest) | The Bilge / The Underrock folk | Inside the islands' underbellies + below noble ships' decks | Engines and sometimes the blood. *Some know, most don't.* |\n| 2 | Tradeworker class *(Aria's class)* | On the islands' upper tiers | Their work; surface is rumour |\n| 3 | Merchant class | On islands; some own trade vessels | The surface, routinely. They KNOW Aurum is part of a larger world (they trade with Solis and Tyramare). |\n| 4 | Minor noble houses | Smaller flying ship-homes | Everything from above; choose what to acknowledge |\n| 5 | Upper nobles | Larger flying ship-homes | The whole game from on high |\n| 6 (highest) | The Royal House | The grandest sky-vessel; the apex of the sky | Everything. *They are the truth-keepers and the executors.* |\n\nThe genius of Aurum's class architecture is that nobody has to lie to anyone in the tiers above or below — each tier simply does not see what the others see. The Underrock knows what holds the city up. The Royal House knows why. The four tiers in between live in a comfortable blur of trade, gossip, and gilded daylight, never asking either question.\n\n═══ AURUM'S SACRED TREE — THE DÌWǓ PAULOWNIA, VIRTUE OF HOPE ═══\n\nAurum holds one of the eight Sacred Trees of Avalúne — the **Dìwǔ (地梧) paulownia,** the Chinese mythological phoenix-roosting tree. Its sacred virtue is **HOPE.**\n\nThe Dìwǔ is rooted at the centre of the original subterranean lake that the ancestors lifted with the land. That centre is now the **Royal Island** — the largest of all the floating islands. The Royal Family lives ON the Hope-tree's heart. The throne sits over the dying tree.\n\nThe tree is being drained. Blood magic feeds on it as a magical battery to keep the islands aloft. It is corrupted, grieving, and only one being in Aurum can still hear its true voice under the blood-corruption — Mínhuì. (See `aurum-minhui` and `aurum-bloodmagic`.)\n\nFour layers compose the Dìwǔ's nature, all canon:\n1. **Original purpose — Tree of Reincarnation / willing sacrifice.** Planted to be fed by offered devotion. The sky cities float because the founders' covenant gave it freely.\n2. **Corruption — blood magic.** Aurum discovered they could STEAL life faster than it could be offered. The shortcut became method.\n3. **Consciousness — primordial sacred tree.** It is alive and aware. It knows. It grieves. It tries to speak.\n4. **Phoenix fragment — Fèngzhé's essence.** The phoenix god left a fragment of himself in the tree before he was forced to flee. The tree is his sacred roosting place. The fragment is the anchor that makes his return possible.\n\nThe Dìwǔ is held in the **Inner Sanctum of the Aurum Archival** — a massive library built around the tree to hide it and preserve it. (See `aurum-culture` for the Archival; `aurum-minhui` for the silent caretaker who works there.)\n\n═══ THE VIRTUE BINDING — AURUM AND ARIA ═══\n\nAria carries HOPE among The Virtues. Aurum holds the HOPE-tree. **Aria is the prophecy child of Aurum's dying Hope-tree.** When she heals the tree, she heals her own birthplace. Saga-grade structural binding — the realm she was born into and the virtue she carries are the SAME thing in two forms.\n\nIf the Dìwǔ finally dies, Aurum falls from the sky. Hundreds of thousands of lives are at stake. Aria's healing of the tree is also the healing of the realm she was born into.\n\n═══ THE SAGA'S STAKE ═══\n\nAurum is the realm Aria was born into. Its corruption IS the corruption she will heal. The blood-magic horror under the gilded surface, the centuries-old Shi Sect High Chancellor manipulating the throne, the dying Hope-tree at the heart of it all, the silent fox goddess who is Aria's mother working as a librarian a few corridors away from the tree — every saga thread converges on Aurum.\n\nWhen Aria returns home as the prophecy child, she walks into a city that will not recognise her, ruled by a Royal House that does not know it has been conned for centuries, served by an Underrock that has been waiting silently for someone to remember they exist. She walks through the Aurum Archival's hallways of knowledge to find what her mother has been silently tending. She touches the Dìwǔ tree, and a phoenix-fragment in its core recognises the fire in her blood. Her mother — sixteen years of silence ending in one moment — finally approaches.\n\nAurum is where the saga goes home.
