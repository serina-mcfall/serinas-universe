SECOND PRINCE CHÉN WǓ (辰武) — \"MARTIAL STAR\"\nHead of the Imperial Guard. Second son of Emperor Chén Yuán and Empress Lí Wǎn. Younger brother to Crown Prince Chén Jǐng. Elder brother to Chén Qīng (concealed as Yōu Lán) and Princess Chén Lán.\n\nThis entry is PARKED at Q1. The composite is locked. Every single one is a man who SHOULD have been closer to the throne than he is. Every one of them was ROBBED. And every one of them fights with steel for a living — warriors, not courtiers.\n\n**🌒 Prince Nan Heng — *A Dream Within a Dream*. The Exiled Boy Who Came Back as Death.** The 7th Prince, exiled to the cold palace as a child after his mother the empress died, abandoned by his father, left to grow up without warmth or safety. Rebuilt himself as a weapon. The army calls him the God of Death. Emotionally dysregulated, possessive, ruthless — a man who takes and says *\"she can't stop me from doing what I want\"* — and underneath, a small abandoned child still waiting for someone to come for him. Walking red flag wearing a crown. *What he brings to Wǔ:* childhood wound. Exile. The prince who became dangerous because the palace threw him away. (Note: this archetype was also a layer in Chén Yuán's composite. Father and son share a wound architecture. Wǔ inherits from his father not the steady-granite but the abandoned-child-who-became-a-weapon energy.)\n\n**⚔️ Xiao Jue — *Legend of the Female General*. The Noble General with a Family Tragedy.** Son of the Xiao army commander — an aristocratic military family brought down by some kind of tragedy. Grew up at Xianchang Academy. Became General Fengyun — one of the youngest and most gifted commanders in the realm. Cold. Precise. Suspicious. Tests loyalty repeatedly before he trusts. But once he lets himself love, he is unbearably tender. Draws her eyebrows for her. Teases her about blushing. Tells her in the middle of battle that with her still around he can't die. He wields a sword for his country and his bloodline — fighting both external enemies and the truth of who destroyed his family. *What he brings to Wǔ:* noble military bloodline. A family's fall to investigate. The prince-coded general whose love is quiet until it is everything.\n\n**🗡️ Chu Bei Jie — *General and I*. The Hidden Half-Brother of the King.** Most beloved general of the Kingdom of Jin and the king's closest strategist. But his real identity is a secret: he is the king's half-brother. His mother killed herself to protect him and left him under the Dowager Empress's care. Only a handful of people know. He serves the throne as a general and a subject — but he is, by blood, a prince who could have been king. Principled, steadfast, utterly loyal to country and to the woman he loves. *What he brings to Wǔ:* hidden royal blood. A mother who died to keep the secret safe. A man who is a prince in fact but a general in title — and whose loyalty is the only throne he will claim.\n\n**🏹 Feng Sui Ge — *Fated Hearts*. The First Prince Being Pushed Out.** Technically First Prince of Susha — but narratively the second-prince archetype: his stepmother Empress Zhuang is quietly positioning her own son Cheng Yang to take the throne. His mother was murdered by her. His father the emperor is weak and compromised. Sui Ge is called the Killer God — cold-blooded on the battlefield, commander of the Feng Battalion, feared across two kingdoms. He fights to keep what should already be his. *What he brings to Wǔ:* the displaced heir. The one whose stepmother killed his mother. A man who rules through fear because he was never allowed to rule through birthright.\n\n**🕯️ Shen Du — *Melody of Golden Age*. The White Impermanence with His Father's Name to Clear.** Grand Chief of the Imperial Guard in a kingdom where the emperor is a baby and the Grand Empress Dowager rules as regent. The common people call him **White Impermanence (白无常)** — a death-god figure from Chinese folklore who comes to collect souls. Cold. Ruthless. Precise. **The wound that defines him:** the Shen family was destroyed when he was a teenager. His father was accused of treason and executed. Shen Du himself was imprisoned as a boy. He survived, clawed his way into the Empress Dowager's favor, became her most feared enforcer — and the whole time, every case he solved, every power play he made, every throat he cut was in service of a single secret goal: **clear his father's name.** He became the machine's sharpest tooth to dig at the evidence that would expose the Prime Minister who framed his father. *What he brings to Wǔ:* a dead father whose name must be cleared. A destroyed noble house he is the last survivor of. Service to the throne that looks loyal from the outside but is vengeance from the inside. A childhood where softness would have killed him. One person from his past who remembers him soft.\n\n═══ THE STRUCTURAL INSIGHT — AOLI HAS FLIPPED THE TROPE ═══\n\n**Every single one of Wǔ's five composite figures is a man who SHOULD have been closer to the throne than he is.
