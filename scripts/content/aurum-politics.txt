AURUM — POWER AND CONTROL\n\n═══ THE ONE-LINE TRUTH ═══\n\nAurum's politics are arranged by ALTITUDE — the higher you fly, the more you matter. Six tiers, six different versions of the truth, one Royal House (and its centuries-old Chancellor) holding the whole structure together by making sure each tier only ever sees what it is supposed to see.\n\n═══ THE SIX-TIER CLASS SYSTEM ═══\n\nClass in Aurum is not abstract. It is LITERAL elevation. Where you live in the sky tells the realm — and yourself — exactly who you are.\n\n| Tier | Class | Lives | Sees |\n|---|---|---|---|\n| 1 (lowest) | The Bilge / The Underrock folk | Inside the islands' underbellies + below noble ships' decks | Engines and sometimes the blood. *Some know, most don't.* |\n| 2 | Tradeworker class *(Aria's class)* | On the islands' upper tiers | Their work; surface is rumour |\n| 3 | Merchant class | On islands; some own trade vessels | The surface, routinely. They KNOW Aurum is part of a larger world. |\n| 4 | Minor noble houses | Smaller flying ship-homes | Everything from above; choose what to acknowledge |\n| 5 | Upper nobles | Larger flying ship-homes | The whole game from on high |\n| 6 (highest) | The Royal House | The grandest sky-vessel; the apex of the sky | Everything. *They are the truth-keepers and the executors.* |\n\nThis table is the political map of Aurum. Every other political fact follows from it.\n\n═══ STATUS BY ALTITUDE ═══\n\nThe basic principle is rigid and visible to everyone every day. The higher your home, the higher your status. Your address IS your rank.\n\n**The Underrock (Tier 1)** lives below the daylight tier of every island, inside the rock-bodies of the islands themselves. They are physically the LOWEST in the sky. They are also the only tier that never leaves the islands' rock — they exist below everyone, always. Their altitude reads as no-altitude to the upper tiers; they may as well be on the surface their ancestors fled.\n\n**Tradeworkers (Tier 2)** live on the islands' middle and upper tiers, in the daylight, in the pagoda neighbourhoods and workshop quarters. They are above the Underrock literally and socially. They do not own air-vessels of their own; they walk the islands' streets, work at their workshops, raise their families in district housing. They are Aurum's craftspeople, smiths, weavers, brewers, tea-merchants, scribes, paper-makers, lantern-makers, the daily-life backbone of the realm. **Aria is born into this tier.**\n\n**Merchants (Tier 3)** also live on the islands' upper tiers, often in the most desirable pagoda quarters with views of the engineered waterfalls. The wealthiest merchants OWN air-vessels — trade ships that move between Aurum's constellation and the realms below. The merchant tier is the BRIDGE between sky and surface; they are the only tier below the nobility who routinely see the rotting world the ancestors fled. *They know Aurum is part of a larger world. They keep this knowledge close.*\n\n**Minor nobility (Tier 4)** lives in flying ship-homes — smaller than the upper-noble vessels, but mobile, personal, status-saturated. A minor noble's ship is their address. They drift between social engagements, dock at the Royal Island for court, and spend their idle days at preferred altitudes for the views. They are above the islands literally — they look down on the daylight tiers without ever setting foot in them unless ceremony requires.\n\n**Upper nobility (Tier 5)** lives in larger flying ship-homes — small palaces in the sky, with retinues of servants, cooks, gardeners (yes, they grow food in shipboard greenhouses), and the inevitable Underrock crew that runs the ship's blood-fed engines below decks. The upper-noble vessels are the great houses of Aurum society. Their drifting between altitudes is itself a status performance — only the upper nobles can afford the fuel to move freely.\n\n**The Royal House (Tier 6)** lives on the GRANDEST SKY-VESSEL — a junk-style flying ship-city as much as a vessel, anchored above the Royal Island, ceremonial and operational at once. Their altitude is the highest in the realm, by tradition and by enforcement. *No noble vessel may dock above the Royal vessel.* The Royal House is, by convention and by sky-law, always the apex.\n\n═══ HOW DECISIONS GET MADE ═══\n\nThe formal political structure of Aurum is a constitutional triad:\n\n**1. The Royal House** — the executive. The monarch issues edicts, presides over court, approves or denies the policies brought by the Chancellor and the noble council. The Royal House holds the secret (the blood magic, the ancestors' covenant gone wrong, the system that keeps the islands aloft) and rules with that secret as the unspoken context of every decision they make.\n\n**2. The High Chancellor** — the chief advisor. In theory, the Chancellor's role is to administer policy on the Royal House's behalf, manage the noble council, and maintain the ritual machinery that keeps the realm aloft. In practice, **the Chancellor is Guì**, has been for centuries, and runs the apparatus she designed. She is the most powerful single figure in the realm and the throne does not know she has been the same woman the entire time. (See `aurum-royalhouse`.)\n\n**3. The Noble Council** — drawn from the upper-noble flying-ship houses. Each great noble house holds a hereditary seat. They debate policy, approve taxes, ratify trade agreements, and provide the social-political weight that lets the Royal House govern without constant friction. The Noble Council is theatrical — most of its real decisions have been pre-shaped by the Chancellor before any debate begins.\n\nDecision flow in normal practice:\n- The Chancellor identifies a problem (a descent flutter, a trade dispute, a corrupted lower noble, an outsider negotiation)\n- The Chancellor brings a recommended solution to the Royal House privately\n- The monarch approves, refines, or (rarely) rejects\n- The Chancellor presents the solution to the Noble Council as the throne's policy\n- The Council debates, performs its objections, and ratifies\n- The policy becomes law\n\n**The crisis decisions** — anything touching the blood-magic system, the descent rate, the Dìwǔ tree, the Underrock — never reach the Council. Those are decided by the Chancellor and the Royal House alone. The Council does not know the secret; therefore the Council is not consulted on the secret.\n\nThe Underrock has NO political representation. They have no seats, no voice in the Council, no formal access to the throne. Their working life is governed by overseers (drawn from the Tradeworker class) who manage the engine crews. Their grievances rise through overseer reports — most of which never leave the desks of mid-rank functionaries. (See `aurum-underrock` for the silent network of resistance the Underrock maintains in the absence of any formal voice.)\n\n═══ THE COURT RHYTHM — WHEN NOBLE SHIPS DOCK VS SCATTER ═══\n\nAurum's politics moves on a court rhythm determined by when the noble ship-homes dock at the Royal Island.\n\n**COURT IN SESSION.** Several times a year — for the great ceremonial periods, the equinoxes, the realm's high festivals, the annual budget review, and any crisis the Chancellor calls — the upper noble flying ship-homes converge on the Royal Island. They dock around the great Royal vessel, gangways extending to the island's upper tiers, mooring lines tethered in a constellation around the central authority. The Royal Island swells. Pagoda quarters fill with noble retinues. Markets bloom around the docked vessels selling delicacies the static-island merchants stock specifically for court days.\n\nDuring court session, the realm's politics happens at compressed intensity. The Council convenes daily. Marriages are arranged. Trade contracts are ratified. Court intrigues run hot. The Chancellor moves between vessels carrying private words. The monarch holds public audiences and private councils. *Everything that matters happens in those weeks.*\n\nViewed from below, the Royal Island in court session looks like a flower in bloom — the central great vessel and its anchored island, surrounded by petals of noble vessels at varied altitudes, the whole structure catching the gilded light. Tradeworkers and merchants who ferry goods up to court vessels for those weeks earn more than they make all year.\n\n**COURT ADJOURNED.** When ceremony ends and policy is set, the noble vessels CAST OFF and scatter. Each upper-noble house drifts to its own preferred altitude — some near the trade routes for visibility, some over remote islands for privacy, some high in the cold air to be seen from below. The Royal Island returns to bare grandeur. The pagoda quarters empty. The markets shrink. The Royal vessel sits silent above the silent Archival above the silent tree above the silent water.\n\nThe scatter is itself political. *Where* an upper-noble vessel drifts after court tells the realm what alliance they are signalling. To drift near a known rival's preferred altitude is provocation. To drift toward a known ally is endorsement. The constellation of noble ships across the realm is a slow-moving political map that updates between court sessions.\n\n**Minor noble ships** follow this rhythm at smaller scale — they dock and scatter alongside the upper houses, adjusting their drift to track the patrons they serve.\n\n**Merchant trade** continues at all altitudes year-round, but slows during court session because the high-traffic routes around the Royal Island become congested with noble vessels. Merchants schedule their long surface-trade voyages around the court calendar.\n\n**The Underrock** does not feel the court rhythm at all. The engines roar continuously regardless of what the upper tiers are doing. The court flowering above the islands does not change anything in the dim chambers below.\n\n═══ THE INFORMATION ASYMMETRY — EACH TIER SEES A DIFFERENT VERSION OF THE TRUTH ═══\n\nThis is the genius — and the rot — of Aurum's political system. **No tier has to lie to the tier above or below it. Each tier simply does not see what the others see.** The control is structural, not narrative. The truth is fragmented by altitude.\n\n**What the Underrock sees:** The engines. The bioluminescent reservoir. The dim chambers. The early deaths of friends and family. The brass-and-steam mechanisms they tend daily. Some Underrock workers — particularly the body-keepers, some of the oldest stokers, the ones who pay attention — KNOW about the blood magic. Most suspect something. The silent network of those who know holds the truth quietly because speaking aloud is not safe. The Underrock sees the SHADOW of what holds the realm aloft. They feel it in the air they breathe.\n\n**What the Tradeworker class sees:** Their work. Their pagoda neighbourhood. The festivals. The waterfalls. The airships overhead. The Underrock as a dim presence below their floorboards — workers seen briefly when business takes them to the lower tiers, goggled and quiet, easy to look away from. The surface? RUMOUR. The Tradeworker tier has been raised to believe the surface is the rotting world the ancestors fled, dangerous and forbidden. They never go down. They never want to. **Aria's worldview, before the saga begins.**\n\n**What the Merchant class sees:** Everything the Tradeworker class sees, PLUS the surface — routinely. Merchants own trade vessels. They go down to Solis ports, to Tyramare guild-houses. They speak with ground-folk. They KNOW Aurum is part of a larger world. They keep this knowledge close because it is socially valuable to them: their access to the surface is what makes them merchants. They also see more of the Underrock than the Tradeworkers do — they need engine work done on their vessels, they negotiate with overseers, they sometimes glimpse the chambers most Tradeworkers never see. Some merchants suspect the blood magic. Most do not pursue the suspicion.\n\n**What the Minor Nobility sees:** Everything the Merchants see, PLUS the political layer. They sit in on Council debates. They hear the Chancellor's policy framings. They understand who is rising and who is falling at court. They see the realm as a GAME. The blood magic? They have heard the term. They have been trained never to ask too publicly. Some know. Some do not. Most have decided, quietly, not to find out.\n\n**What the Upper Nobility sees:** Everything the Minor Nobility sees, PLUS access to the throne. Upper noble houses are admitted to private council with the monarch. They KNOW the realm runs on something extraordinary. The most senior upper nobles know the broad shape of the blood magic — they are not told the operational details, but they have been told enough to understand why the realm cannot openly acknowledge how it stays aloft. They are complicit by knowledge. They protect the secret because their own status depends on the realm not falling.\n\n**What the Royal House sees:** Everything. Every layer. The blood magic. The Underrock's role. The Dìwǔ tree's drained state. The ancestors' original covenant. The descent the realm would suffer if the system stopped. The full operational details of Guì's apparatus. They are the truth-keepers and the executors — the only tier that holds the whole picture. *And they do not know that the woman who runs the apparatus for them has been the same woman for centuries, wearing different faces, sent each time by the Shi Sect that controls the secret she introduced.* (See `aurum-royalhouse`.)\n\nThe Royal House thinks they sit at the top of the information pyramid. **Guì sits above them.** She is the only being in Aurum who sees ALL of it — every tier, every truth, the whole game. The Royal House does not know there is another tier above their own.\n\n**The political genius of this asymmetry:** every tier has REASONS to keep the system as it is. The Underrock keeps quiet because speaking is dangerous. The Tradeworkers do not see the secret. The Merchants protect their access to the surface and their positions. The Nobility protect their status. The Royal House protects the realm. **Nobody at any tier has both the knowledge AND the freedom to act.** Until Aria.
