AURUM — DAYLIGHT CULTURE\n\n═══ THE ONE-LINE TRUTH ═══\n\nAbove the cloud layer, Aurum is gilded. Pagoda neighbourhoods stack along island tiers; brass-and-jade engineering threads through every wall; engineered waterfalls pour off every island's edge; airships drift between islands carrying merchants and minor nobles in trailing silk. The whole realm is sunset-lit and beautiful. The horror beneath the rock is not visible from any market on any island.\n\n═══ ASIAN PAGODA + VICTORIAN STEAMPUNK FUSED ═══\n\nThe defining aesthetic of daylight Aurum is the FUSION of two architectural traditions that never met on any other continent.\n\n**Pagoda forms** give the buildings their silhouette — tiered upturned eaves, layered rooflines, lacquered timber posts, paper-screen walls in the gentler quarters, courtyards opening onto inner gardens. The shapes are recognisably descended from the Aoli architectural family but carried up into the sky and adapted to floating-island conditions over centuries.\n\n**Victorian-steampunk engineering** is FUSED INTO the pagoda forms — not stacked beside them. Brass clockwork is integrated into the timber framing. Steam-pipes run through walls and emerge as decorative elements. Pneumatic-tube messaging systems link upper-tier pagodas to neighbouring buildings. Mechanical lifts within multi-story pagoda complexes rise on brass-and-jade pulley systems. Heat is delivered by steam-radiators built into the original wall-design. Even the lacquer on a noble pagoda's beams may conceal a brass conduit.\n\nThe HALLMARK is invisibility-by-integration: the engineering does not LOOK steampunk in the cluttered Victorian sense. It looks Asian — the brass elements are designed to read as ornament, the pipes as decorative bands, the gauges as wall-art, the mechanisms as part of the building's beauty. The realm has had centuries to perfect this fusion. It is a mature, settled, native-feeling style.\n\nThe most distinctive Aurum buildings combine both traditions at scale:\n- Pagoda observatories with great brass clockwork mechanisms in their interiors (the clockwork visible through glass panels)\n- Multi-story merchant pagodas with internal lift systems and steam-heated baths\n- The grand Aurum Archival itself — pagoda silhouette, steampunk infrastructure within, the Inner Sanctum at the heart\n\nThe Royal Palace and the Royal vessel both express the fusion at imperial scale. The Royal vessel in particular — the great pagoda-bearing junk-style sky-vessel — is the visual signature of the realm: pagoda buildings carried atop a ship-form, brass and jade and lacquer all together, drifting through golden-hour light.\n\n═══ THE ENGINEERED WATERFALLS ═══\n\nEvery floating island has waterfalls pouring from its edges into the cloud layer below. These are part aesthetic, part engineering, part necessity.\n\n**The water source** is each island's bioluminescent reservoir (see `aurum-underrock` for the cosmology of the reservoirs). Reservoir water is constantly replenished by some mechanism the magic-banned realm does not publicly understand — likely connected to the original lifting magic that distributed the subterranean lake. The water needs SOMEWHERE to go to keep the reservoir from overflowing. The waterfalls are that somewhere.\n\n**The engineering** is Underrock work, mostly invisible from the daylight tiers. Channels carved into the rock direct water from the reservoirs to overflow points at the islands' edges. Brass valves regulate the flow. The waterfalls' apparent gentleness or thunderousness is calibrated by the Underrock plumbers; some islands have tunable falls that can be brightened for festivals.\n\n**The aesthetic effect from above** is the realm's signature. From a sky-deck on any island, looking out, the eye sees other islands at varying altitudes, each trailing silver-white waterfalls into the cloud layer. The light catches the falls in late afternoon and turns them gold. From below the cloud layer, the falls appear and disappear as the clouds shift — surface-folk who have seen Aurum from a Solis port describe it as heaven leaking water.\n\n**Practical use** of the falling water happens at multiple points:\n- Hydropower stations (mostly Underrock-managed) tap the falls for mechanical work\n- Decorative cascades within the daylight tiers re-direct portions of the falls into pagoda gardens, ornamental ponds, and pagoda-courtyard fountains\n- The mist from the falls is a natural climate-tempering for the islands' lower flanks\n\nThe waterfalls are also CULTURALLY central. Children are taught to watch them. Lovers meet at fall-viewing platforms. Poets write about them. The realm's most famous song-cycle is *The Twelve Falls of the Year,* a series of compositions describing how the falls catch light differently in each lunar month.\n\n═══ GREENERY ON THE ISLANDS — NATIVE LAND CARRIED UP ═══\n\nThe islands' lush vegetation is not imported. It is the ORIGINAL FLORA of the lifted continent — the trees, grasses, mosses, herbs, and shrubs that grew on the land-pieces the ancestors tore from the surface.\n\nWhat this means in practice:\n- **Native trees** — varieties no longer found below in the corrupted continental soil. The islands carry an ARK of the pre-corruption ecosystem. Botanists in Solis would weep to see what grows in an Aurum public garden.\n- **Heirloom crops** — the pagoda neighbourhoods' kitchen gardens grow rice, vegetables, herbs, and fruits whose strains have not been touched by corruption. Aurum food is literally PURER than surface food.\n- **Native birdlife** — songbirds carried up with the trees centuries ago, now thriving in island canopies. Some bird-species exist nowhere else.\n- **Cultivated gardens** — the pagoda quarters interlace public gardens, koi-pond gardens, contemplative rock-and-moss gardens, and the noble pagoda private gardens. Gardening is one of the realm's high arts.\n\nThe greenery is also intensely AESTHETICALLY CURATED. Centuries of garden-tradition shape the islands' planting; even wild-looking corners are wild by intention. Maintenance is constant. Garden-staff are widespread across all daylight tiers — gardening is a respected craft, often a hereditary one.\n\nThe upper tiers of every island are GREEN. The islands appear, from a distance, as green-and-gold formations trailing white falls. The colour palette of Aurum is essentially: green (the gardens), gold (the sunset light on every surface), silver-white (the falls), and brass-and-jade (the architecture).\n\n═══ DAILY LIFE OF THE TRADEWORKER CLASS — ARIA'S CLASS ═══\n\nAria was born into the Tradeworker class. Her daily life — and the daily life of millions of people like her across the islands — is the SOCIAL HEART of daylight Aurum.\n\n**Where they live:** the upper tiers of the islands, in pagoda neighbourhoods organised by craft. Every district has its specialty — weaver districts, smith districts, paper districts, lantern districts, tea-merchant districts. Apartments are above shops; workshops open onto the streets. The district has its own small temple-shrines, its own community well-fountain (often a re-directed waterfall trickle), its own market square.\n\n**The work:** the trades that keep daylight Aurum running. Smiths work brass and steel for the realm's machinery. Weavers produce silk and cotton in styles unique to Aurum. Paper-makers produce the paper that Aurum's bureaucracy and culture run on. Lantern-makers produce both the practical lights of the realm and the gorgeous ceremonial lanterns of festival days. Brewers produce rice wine and tea. Scribes produce documents, letters, and the cheaper editions of literature the upper tiers commission. Tea merchants run the small daily-life tea shops.\n\nA tradeworker's day starts early. The workshop opens at first light. Children old enough to apprentice (eight, ten, twelve) work alongside parents. Midday brings a meal at home or at a shop near the workshop. Afternoon work continues. The shop closes at dusk. Evening is for family, for the tea house, for the small social rounds of the district.\n\n**The culture:** community-tight, district-based, craft-proud. A weaver knows other weavers. A lantern-maker's child plays with another lantern-maker's child. Marriages within the craft district are common. Disputes within the district are usually resolved at the district level, never reaching the magistrates.\n\nTradeworkers eat well by realm-historical standards. Rice, vegetables, fish, occasional meat, fresh fruit from the island gardens, tea every morning and evening. They wear simple but well-made cotton in working hours and silk for festivals. They live in dwellings warmer and dryer than any surface-folk equivalent. They are LITERATE — the Tradeworker tier values learning; the district scribes are also informal teachers; children learn enough characters to read trade documents and the cheaper literature.\n\n**What they don't see:** the surface (it is rumour to them), the Underrock (a dim presence below; they know workers exist but rarely think about them), the political machinations of the upper tiers (above their concern), the truth of the blood magic (most have never heard the term).\n\n**What they DO see, sometimes:** the Underrock workers passing through on errands. The minor noble vessels overhead. The realm's festivals, all of which they attend. The Royal House's ceremonial appearances on the great vessel, glimpsed at distance, always commented on at the family table.\n\n**Aria's specific corner of this** — see `aurum-ariaorigin`.\n\n═══ DAILY LIFE OF THE MERCHANT CLASS — THE BRIDGE TO THE SURFACE ═══\n\nThe Merchant class lives one tier up from the Tradeworkers — same daylight, sometimes the same pagoda neighbourhoods, but with vessel-ownership and surface access that distinguishes them sharply.\n\n**Where they live:** the most desirable pagoda quarters with views of the engineered waterfalls. Larger residences than the tradeworkers — multi-courtyard pagoda complexes, sometimes with their own small private gardens. The wealthiest merchants have their own dock at the island's edge for their trade vessels.\n\n**The work:** trade. Some merchants run inter-island routes within Aurum's constellation; the wealthier ones run trade vessels DOWN to the surface — to Solis ports primarily, with secondary routes to Tyramare. The trade is the core of their wealth and the basis of their social power. They bring up surface goods (raw metals, surface crops the islands cannot grow, exotic textiles, certain medicines, books from the Solis presses) and bring down Aurum goods (the realm's silks, paper, brass-craft, lacquerware, tea blends).\n\n**The vessels:** merchant trade vessels are smaller than noble flying-ships but distinct in design — built for cargo, with smaller passenger quarters and large holds. A wealthy merchant's vessel is a working ship that doubles as a status display when in port.\n\n**The culture:** cosmopolitan, well-travelled, slightly suspect to the more parochial Tradeworker tier (\"merchants know too much\") and slightly looked-down-upon by the nobility (\"merchants smell of the surface\"). Merchants have their own clubs, their own tea-houses, their own marriage circles. They marry across districts and sometimes across islands — the merchant tier is the most mobile social tier outside the nobility.\n\n**They KNOW Aurum is part of a larger world.** They have seen Solis port-cities. They have walked surface markets. They have met surface-folk and traded with them. They keep this knowledge close because it is socially valuable. The Tradeworker tier mostly does not believe their stories; the noble tiers do not need to be told.\n\n**The merchant tier is also where Aurum first finds out about world events.** A merchant returning from Solis might bring back word of a coup, a famine, a new fashion, a new philosophy. The merchant tea-houses are the realm's news circuits. By the time a piece of surface news reaches the noble council, it has already been chewed over in three merchant tea-houses and a tradeworker district market.\n\n═══ SUNSET-CODED MOOD — AURUM IS GILDED ═══\n\nThe defining MOOD of daylight Aurum is sunset. Not literal sunset — though the realm holds its grandest ceremonial moments at golden hour — but the sunset-coded *quality* that pervades every visual element, every social ritual, every cultural product.\n\n**The light** above the cloud layer is exceptionally gold. The atmosphere holds the warm wavelengths longer than the surface does, possibly because of the altitude and the cleaner air. Every hour of the day looks like a slightly different version of golden hour. Midday on Aurum is cooler-gold; afternoon is warm-gold; evening is deep amber. The realm's painters work almost entirely in gold-warm palettes.\n\n**The mood** is the same — slow, unhurried, ceremonial, beautiful. Time moves more slowly in Aurum than in the surface realms. Decisions are made over weeks rather than days. Court ceremonies last hours. Tea is poured with formality. Greetings are extended. Even the merchants, the busiest tier, move at a pace that would be considered languid in Solis.\n\n**The aesthetic SIGNATURE:** gold. Gold-leaf on noble pagoda eaves. Gold thread in formal hanfu. Gold-lacquer on tea ware. Gold-tipped writing brushes. Gold reflections in still water. The realm is named for gold and presents gold relentlessly. *Aurum = gold; the realm wears its name on every surface.*\n\n**The shadow underneath** — the realm is built on horror. The gold is real but the foundation is rotten. The gilded mood is cultivated, deliberate, and (at its highest tiers) consciously maintained as a kind of collective denial. *Beautiful and built on horror.* The contradiction is the realm's deepest truth, and the daylight tiers maintain it by simply not looking down.\n\n═══ TRADE WITH SOLIS AND TYRAMARE ═══\n\nAurum's connection to the surface is the merchant trade. The two trade partners are Solis (primary) and Tyramare (secondary).\n\n**Solis** — the Tang-coded continental capital of cosmopolitan excess — is Aurum's natural counterpart on the surface. Both are wealthy, both are aesthetic, both run on trade and luxury. The Solis port-cities receive Aurum trade vessels several times a season. The trade is mutually enriching: Solis silk, jade, porcelain, books, perfumes go up; Aurum brass-craft, paper, tea blends, exotic dried goods (some of which are uniquely Aurum because of the island-grown heirloom strains) come down.\n\nThe Solis nobility considers Aurum a fascinating eccentric — an ally, not quite a peer (Solis is older), and a useful market. The Solis pleasure quarters know Aurum merchants well; the most prosperous Solis houses have Aurum-merchant patrons.\n\nThere is one other Solis-Aurum link the realms do not publicly know. **Lady Pearl** (the immortal Water Twin, hiding as a pleasure-house madam in Solis) maintains a quiet awareness of Aurum trade through the merchants who pass through her house. Her divine sister Mínhuì hides in Aurum. The merchant trade is, among other things, the conduit through which two divine siblings indirectly keep contact across the continent. (Whether they exchange messages this way is open canon.)\n\n**Tyramare** — the seven-guild-district mercantile city — is the secondary trade partner. The Tyramare merchant guilds receive Aurum vessels at their port and trade vigorously, but the relationship is more transactional than the Solis bond. Tyramare buys Aurum goods to resell across its trade network; Aurum sees Tyramare primarily as a redistribution market. The Solenthera priesthood of Tyramare (the Shi-aligned cult) takes a quiet interest in Aurum trade for its own reasons — the Entheras knows Guì is in Aurum, and the trade flow is one of the channels through which the Shi network maintains coordination.\n\nNo other realms trade significantly with Aurum. Maramir is too distant. Braedun and Isenholt are too closed. Aoli has no formal trade relationship with Aurum (the imperial court does not officially recognise the sky cities, treating them as a curiosity rather than a peer realm). The Forgotten Isles and the Sunken Sea have no trade.\n\nThe trade routes themselves are part of Aurum's culture. The merchants who run them are admired in their districts. The schedules are celebrated — the autumn fleet departing for Solis, the spring fleet returning from Tyramare. Children grow up watching the trade vessels rise and fall along the cloud layer. *For most of daylight Aurum, the merchant trade is the only contact with the surface they will ever know.*
