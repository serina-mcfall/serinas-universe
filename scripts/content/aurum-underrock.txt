AURUM — THE UNDERROCK\n\n═══ THE ONE-LINE TRUTH ═══\n\nThe lowest caste of Aurum lives carved into the rock-bodies of the floating islands themselves, in a dim bioluminescent world that still remembers it was once a single subterranean lake. Above, they are dismissed as \"the Bilge.\" Among themselves, with dignity, they are the Underrock. They tend the engines that hold the realm aloft. Some of them know what the engines actually run on. None of them are allowed to say.\n\n═══ THE TWO-NAME DYNAMIC — \"THE BILGE\" VS \"THE UNDERROCK\" ═══\n\nAurum has two names for its lowest tier and the place that lowest tier lives. **Each name is a worldview.**\n\n**\"The Bilge\"** is what the upper classes call them. The word is dismissive — bilge-water is the foul stagnant water that pools in a ship's lowest hold; calling people \"the Bilge\" treats them as the sediment of the realm. It is what minor nobles say to each other when they need to gesture at the engine workers without thinking too hard. It is the word the Royal Court uses in formal documents that need a polite-sounding term for the labour-tier without admitting the tier is a labour-tier. The word lets the upper tiers not see the people; it lets them see only the function.\n\n**\"The Underrock\"** is what the people who live there call themselves. The word is dignified — the rock is the LAND the ancestors lifted into the sky; the rock is the BODY of the islands; to live in the rock is to live in the realm's foundation. To call themselves Underrock is to claim their place in the realm's structure, not to deny it. The word is older than the Bilge; the Bilge was an upper-class import that arrived sometime after the blood-magic system began. The Underrock is what the people called themselves before they were dismissed.\n\n**Each time the two names appear in dialogue, the saga teaches the reader who looks down on whom.** The moment Aria — born to the Tradeworker class, raised to use \"Bilge\" without thinking — first hears one of them say \"Underrock\" instead, and SEES the dignity in it, is when her worldview shifts. (See `aurum-ariaorigin`.)\n\nThe convention is rigid in the upper tiers. A noble who used \"Underrock\" in court would be marked as eccentric or sympathetic. A merchant who used it in the markets would be teased by their peers but probably not punished. A Tradeworker who used it would be considered odd. **Inside the Underrock, \"Bilge\" is never used.** It is a word from above and stays above.\n\n═══ GOGGLES AT AGE 6 — RITE OF PASSAGE ═══\n\nUnderrock children are born with normal eyes. They live their first six years in the dim chambers of the Underrock without protection — children's eyes adapt naturally, and small ones do not yet do the work that requires shielding.\n\n**At age six, every Underrock child is fitted with their first set of brass goggles.** The fitting is a rite of passage. The family gathers. An elder (often a goggle-maker, sometimes a grandmother who has done this for every child in the family for generations) presents the goggles. The leather strap is sized to the child's head. The lenses are tinted to filter the bioluminescent reservoir-light to a comfortable level for working eyes. The child puts them on. The family applauds.\n\nThe goggles mark the moment a child becomes **a worker-in-waiting.** From this moment on, the child is permitted to enter the engine rooms, the deeper chambers, the working corridors. They begin their apprenticeship — not formally, but in the way Underrock children always have: at a parent's elbow, watching, fetching, learning by being there.\n\nThe goggles are never taken off in working hours after age six. They become part of the body. By the time an Underrock child is fifteen, the leather has shaped to the bridge of their nose. The brass has been polished by their own thumbs a thousand times. The lens-tint has been adjusted twice as their eye has matured.\n\n**Goggles are class-marked at first sight.** When an Underrock worker comes up to the daylight tiers — and it is rare — they wear darker-tinted goggles for the journey. The upper tiers see the goggles, register the class, and look away. The looking-away is so habitual that a goggled figure on a daylight street can pass through a market mostly unnoticed. *Daylight folk have learned to look away.*\n\nA goggle-maker is a respected craft inside the Underrock. The family that makes the goggles for a neighbourhood is an honoured family. New designs are passed down. A child's first goggles are simple; an old worker's goggles are intricate, often etched with family marks, replaced lens by lens over a working life.\n\n═══ THE BIOLUMINESCENT RESERVOIR — THE ORIGINAL SUBTERRANEAN LAKE FRAGMENTED ═══\n\n**Serina's verbatim:** *\"The Resovaur was a subterrainan lake when the anscestors tore the land from its roots they uplifted that subterainian lake with it due to the magics used for this endevour and the process each island lifted took a peice of this water source as a part of the floating islands make up so now every island has a small lake and subterainaian river flowing through it.\"*\n\nThe Underrock's defining feature is THE RESERVOIR — a body of bioluminescent water that runs through the rock-body of every island. This is the same water, in a sense, on every island: when the ancestors lifted the land, they unintentionally lifted the vast subterranean lake beneath it, and the lifting fragmented the lake. Every island took a portion. Every island's reservoir is a piece of the same original water, scattered into the sky.\n\nThe water still BEHAVES like one body. What happens in one island's reservoir reverberates faintly through all the others — the reservoir-tenders speak of currents that cannot be local, of the water on one island going still when a storm hits the water on another. The realm's hydrologists (such as the Underrock has) believe the waters are connected through some channel the lifting-magic preserved. Mínhuì, who can hear what others cannot, knows they are right.\n\n**The bioluminescence** comes from the original lake's pre-uplift nature. The water is ancient — older than any human civilization in Avalúne — and it carries its own faint magic in it. That magic appears as a soft blue-green glow that pulses with the water's flow. In a still pool, the glow is steady. In a running channel, the glow is brighter. In a storm-stirred reservoir, the whole chamber lights up. The Underrock is never dark, never bright — it is always lit by the water.\n\n**The waterfalls.** Every engineered waterfall pouring off an island is reservoir-water — the original lake-water leaving its prison. The Underrock has built channels and overflows that direct water to the falls; the falls are aesthetic above and engineered below. From the upper tiers, the waterfalls are beautiful. From the Underrock side, they are part of the daily plumbing — the water-tier knows the falls intimately, manages their flow, listens to them.\n\nThe reservoir is also DRINKING WATER, COOKING WATER, BATHING WATER, and the source of the fish the Underrock eats. It is the heart of the lower tier in every sense.\n\n═══ UNDERROCK JOBS — THE FOUR TIERS WITHIN THE LOWER TIER ═══\n\nWithin the Underrock, work organises into four sub-tiers. These are not class-tiers — they are job-families. An Underrock worker may work in any tier in their lifetime; family lines often span several.\n\n**ENGINE TIER.** The deepest, hottest, hardest-working sub-tier. Three core jobs:\n- **Stokers** — feed the engine cores, keep the boilers hot, keep the steam moving. The most physically punishing job in the realm. Stokers' faces stay reddened from the heat; their hands callus to thick brown leather; their chests broaden. They are the muscle of the Underrock. *Stokers die earliest. The blood magic draws on them most.*\n- **Pipe-fitters** — maintain and repair the brass-and-steam pipework that carries the engines' output through every island. Skilled work; pipe-fitters take long apprenticeships. They know the engines from the inside out.\n- **Body-keepers** — handle the dead. When a stoker (or any Underrock worker) passes, the body-keepers prepare the body. They also conduct the secret ritual that draws the remaining life-force into the apparatus. Body-keepers KNOW. They do not speak of what they know. They are paid more than other Underrock workers — quietly, in extra rations rather than visible coin — and they are watched.\n\n**WATER TIER.** Manages the reservoir and everything that flows.\n- **Reservoir tenders** — care for the bioluminescent water. They check water levels, water clarity, the health of the bioluminescence itself. Often the most spiritually-inclined of the Underrock; the reservoir is sacred to many.\n- **Plumbers** — maintain the channels, valves, overflow systems, and waterfall feeds that move reservoir-water through the islands. They are the unseen keepers of every fountain, every fall, every bath in Aurum.\n- **Fishers** — work the deeper pools where the cave-fish live, providing protein for the Underrock and trade-fish for the upper tiers. The cave-fish have luminous scales; a fisher returning from a good catch carries a basket of moving silver light.\n- **Ferrymen** — operate the small barges that move people and goods along the reservoir's deeper channels. The Underrock has its own waterway system; ferrymen know every current.\n\n**MIDDLE TIER.** The skilled-craft heart of the Underrock.\n- **Mechanics** — beyond the engine pipework, mechanics tend everything that has gears, springs, brass and steel — every clock, every elevator, every machine in the upper tiers' homes that the Underrock built and continues to maintain.\n- **Goggle-makers** — described above. Honoured craft. A goggle-maker's family is a fixed point in a neighbourhood.\n- **Gardeners** — yes, the Underrock has gardens. Bioluminescent moss-gardens, cultivated mushroom plantations, glowing-vine arbors near the reservoir. Beautiful in their dim way.\n- **Mushroom farmers** — the Underrock's main crop after fish. Cultivated in low-light beds, irrigated by reservoir-water, harvested in rotations. Some varieties go up to the daylight tiers as delicacies; the upper tiers do not always know they are eating Underrock food.\n- **Smiths** — work the metal the Underrock needs. Smaller forges than the upper-tier smiths use; precision work rather than scale.\n- **Memory-keepers** — the elder craft. Memory-keepers hold the Underrock's stories, the family lineages, the ritual songs, the names of the dead. Every neighbourhood has at least one. Some are also reservoir tenders or goggle-makers; the work overlaps.\n\n**UPPER TIER.** The interface jobs — Underrock workers who go up.\n- **Bell-ringers** — tend the signal bells that link the Underrock to the upper tiers. Communications work.\n- **Runners** — carry messages between the Underrock and the daylight tiers. Wear the darkest goggles; trained from young to manage the light. Often young, fast, well-spoken.\n- **Door-stewards** — the Underrock workers who staff the doors and gates between the lower and upper levels. They are the visible Underrock to upper-tier visitors.\n- **Goods-handlers** — load and unload at the lifts and gangways that move trade between the Underrock and the daylight tiers. They see more of the upper tiers than most Underrock workers ever do.\n\nA family that has been in the Underrock for generations may have, in one extended household, a stoker grandfather, a memory-keeper grandmother, a pipe-fitter mother, a fisher father, a goggle-maker aunt, a runner cousin, and a child of six just being fitted for her first lenses.\n\n═══ LIVING QUARTERS — CARVED INTO ROCK, TIERED, COMMUNAL HEART AT THE RESERVOIR PLAZA ═══\n\nThe Underrock is the inside of the islands' rock-bodies, hollowed out across centuries into a living warren of dwellings, workshops, and waterways.\n\n**The dwellings** are carved into the rock face — small, snug, each cut directly into the stone. Stone walls are warm in winter, cool in summer; the rock holds an even temperature year-round. Family units are small (a couple, their children, sometimes a grandparent) but communal spaces are large. Doorways open onto shared corridors; corridors open onto shared chambers; chambers open onto the reservoir.\n\n**The dwellings are TIERED** along the rock face — vertical levels of homes accessed by stone stairs that wind up and down the chambers. Some neighbourhoods stretch six or seven tiers above the reservoir level. The poorest dwellings are nearest the engines (warm but loud). The most desirable are nearest the reservoir (cool, quiet, bioluminescent-lit). Family wealth shows in elevation — but at all elevations, the homes are CARVED, the rock is the same stone, and the difference between best and worst is one of comfort, not class.\n\n**Each home has a single warm-amber lantern** mounted by the door. The amber lights are the Underrock's signature — a personal mark, a way to find your home in the dim, a counterpoint to the bioluminescent blue-green of the reservoir. Visitors say the Underrock looks like a starscape in reverse: blue-green water below, amber stars above, both reflecting in the dim corridor walls.\n\n**The Reservoir Plaza** is the social heart of every Underrock community. A wide chamber where the reservoir surfaces; stone stairs descend to a water-edge gathering place; benches carved into the rock face form an amphitheatre around the water. **It is the only OPEN space in the Underrock.** Festivals happen here. Memorial ceremonies happen here. Memory-keepers sing here. Lovers meet here. Children play near the water-edge under the eyes of grandparents who never have to raise their voices because the chamber's acoustics carry every word.\n\nThe Reservoir Plaza is where the Underrock TEACHES ITSELF WHO IT IS. Every Underrock child grows up sitting at the water-edge listening to memory-keepers. Every Underrock adult comes back, again and again, across a lifetime. The Plaza is the cathedral the realm above does not know exists.\n\n═══ BIOLUMINESCENT FLORA AND FAUNA ═══\n\nThe Underrock has its own ecosystem, evolved (or carried up with the original lake) over centuries.\n\n**Flora:**\n- **Glowing mosses** — coat the corridor walls in patches, providing soft natural light. Tended by the moss-gardeners.\n- **Bioluminescent mushrooms** — cultivated in dedicated beds, both for food and for light. Several varieties; the soft-blue oysters are eaten, the pale-green caps are used for lamps.\n- **Glow-vines** — climbing plants that thrive in the reservoir's mist. Used to decorate the Reservoir Plaza on festival days.\n- **Reed-stands** — luminous reeds growing at the reservoir's edge, used for basket-weaving and for the candle-rushes the Underrock burns on memorial days.\n\n**Fauna:**\n- **Cave-fish** — the staple protein. Pale, sometimes translucent, with luminous scales that glow softly in the deep water. Several species, ranging from finger-sized minnows to the broad-flat \"plate-fish\" that feed a family.\n- **Glow-toads** — small amphibians with phosphorescent skin patches. Children sometimes catch them in jars to use as bedside nightlights. Toad-keeping is a children's hobby.\n- **Reservoir-shrimp** — small crustaceans that filter the water. Eaten in stews; also act as the reservoir's living water-quality monitor (when the shrimp dim or die in numbers, the reservoir-tenders know something is wrong).\n- **Cave-bats** — the Underrock has its own bats, smaller than surface bats and less numerous. They roost in the highest corners of the deepest chambers and rarely interact with the people.\n\nThe whole ecosystem is fragile and interconnected. Reservoir-tenders consider it sacred. The arrival of a new species (carried up by an unwitting trader's cargo) is treated as a serious event.\n\n═══ ATMOSPHERIC SENSORY DETAIL ═══\n\nWhat the Underrock is LIKE, sensory:\n\n**SIGHT.** Soft blue-green from every direction (reservoir, mosses, mushrooms), counterpointed by amber lantern-points (the doors). Eyes adjust quickly. Goggled workers see clearly; ungoggled visitors see a hush of light. The chambers feel cathedral-vast in some places and intimate-snug in others — the carved-rock architecture varies dramatically.\n\n**SOUND.** Constant: the hum of the engines, distant in some chambers, nearby in others. The lap of the reservoir water against stone steps. The soft creak of brass mechanisms. Nearer: voices, footsteps on stone, the tap of a goggle-maker's hammer, the hiss of a stew pot. The acoustics carry. A song from one chamber drifts into the next. The whole Underrock sometimes sounds like one continuous murmur.\n\n**SMELL.** Rock — cool, mineral. Damp — never rotten, but always present. Reservoir-water — faintly sweet, faintly mineral, with a hint of green from the mosses. Cooking — fish, mushroom, broth, the occasional baked-grain treat. Engine smell — coal, hot brass, steam — strong near the engine tier, faint elsewhere. Lantern oil — warm, slightly herbal.\n\n**TOUCH.** The rock walls are smooth where many hands have brushed them, rough where they have not. The reservoir water is cool but not cold. The corridors are even-tempered. The engine chambers are HOT — uncomfortably so for visitors. The dwellings are warm and dry.\n\n**TASTE.** Mushroom and fish, mostly. Reservoir-water (sweet, mineral). Mushroom tea (earthy, a luxury). Special-occasion grain breads brought down from the upper tiers. The taste of the Underrock is elemental — water, rock, mushroom, fish, sometimes a fragment of the sun in a stolen apple.\n\n**OVERALL FEELING.** Quiet, dim, alive. The Underrock is not gloomy — it is HUSHED. The light is soft. The air is even. The people are calm. There is a deep peace in the chambers that the upper tiers, with all their gilded sunsets, do not have. *The Underrock is also, in the realm's whole moral architecture, the SANEST tier.* The people who live there have nothing to lie to themselves about.\n\n═══ TRAVEL UP — ALLOWED BUT RARE ═══\n\nUnderrock workers may travel up to the daylight tiers. The journey is permitted. It is also rare.\n\n**Why it is rare:**\n- **The light hurts their eyes.** Even with the darkest goggles, the daylight tiers are intensely bright. Underrock eyes have adapted to the bioluminescence; the sun is a wound. Workers blink violently. They squint. They have learned to never linger.\n- **The upper tiers stare and look away.** The Underrock figure passing through a market is registered as Other and then ignored. The social cost of being looked-at-and-then-unseen is high. Most Underrock workers find the experience humiliating.\n- **The work below does not stop.** Engine shifts are continuous. Reservoir-tending is constant. Family responsibilities pull people back down quickly.\n- **There is little to GO up FOR.** What the Underrock wants from the upper tiers (rare goods, special foods, news) is mostly brought down by the goods-handlers and runners who already make those journeys.\n\n**Who DOES go up:**\n- Runners and goods-handlers, daily, as part of their work.\n- Door-stewards, every shift.\n- The occasional reservoir-tender on ceremonial business.\n- Memory-keepers, very rarely, for cross-island gatherings.\n- A grieving family member visiting an upper-tier acquaintance who once knew their dead.\n- A young person curious enough to risk the embarrassment of being stared at — usually for one trip, never twice.\n\n**The journey itself:** elevators rise from the Underrock corridors to the daylight tiers. Some are operated by Underrock workers; some are mechanical lifts. The transition from amber-and-blue light to gilded sunlight is shocking; even with the darkest goggles, the eye needs minutes to adjust. Most travellers wait at the top of the lift in a small ante-chamber, blinking, before stepping into the daylight market.\n\nThe return is easier. The descent into the Underrock's familiar dim is a relief. Some travellers describe stepping back into the lift as feeling like coming home.\n\n═══ THE SILENT NETWORK — THE UNDERROCK'S QUIET TRUTH-KEEPING RESISTANCE ═══\n\nThe Underrock has no political voice in Aurum. They are not represented on the Noble Council. They have no formal access to the throne. Their grievances rise through overseer reports that mostly never leave mid-rank desks.\n\nWhat they have instead is **the silent network.**\n\nThe ones who KNOW about the blood magic — the body-keepers, the oldest stokers, certain memory-keepers, certain reservoir-tenders who have heard the water grieving — find each other. They do not announce themselves. They do not gather formally. **They cannot speak aloud — tongues are not safe in the Underrock; the overseer system has informers.** But they recognise each other through small signs: a particular phrasing of \"Underrock\" in a memory-song; a particular gesture at a body-keeping ritual; a particular pause when a stoker's name is read at a memorial.\n\nThe network is OLD. It has been running, quietly, for as long as the blood-magic system has been running. Generations of body-keepers have passed the knowledge to the next generation of body-keepers. Generations of memory-keepers have woven the truth into songs that sound like ordinary memorial-songs to anyone who does not know the codes. The network never DOES anything overt — they cannot. They simply KEEP the truth alive, holding it between them, ready for the day someone will be able to act on it.\n\n**Who they are:**\n- Body-keepers (most of them, by the time they have done the work for ten years)\n- The eldest stokers (the ones who survived long enough to notice the pattern of early deaths)\n- Certain memory-keepers (the ones who carry the inherited songs)\n- A handful of reservoir-tenders (the ones the water has spoken to)\n- The very oldest goggle-makers (who have been fitting goggles to working-folk for so long they have seen too many die young)\n- **Possibly Mínhuì herself, on her quietest visits down.** (See `aurum-minhui`.)\n\n**What they do:**\n- Keep the count of the dead\n- Pass the names of the body-kept down generationally so no name is forgotten\n- Hold the songs that carry the truth\n- Recognise each other — and recognise outsiders who might be safe to speak to\n- Wait\n\nWhen Aria comes home — when the prophecy child arrives in Aurum and asks the right question — **the silent network will be the first part of Aurum to answer her.** The Royal House will be confronted. The Chancellor will be unmasked. But the Underrock has been keeping the truth in trust for centuries, and they will be the first ones to recognise that the moment they have waited for has finally come.\n\n*Some of them will weep when they realise they can finally speak. Some of them will simply nod and start telling her everything they have held in silence for their whole working lives.*
