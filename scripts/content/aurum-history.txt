AURUM — THE ANCIENT UPLIFT AND THE SLOW DESCENT\n\n═══ THE ONE-LINE TRUTH ═══\n\nAurum's whole history is one long irony — they fled corruption by tearing the land out of the world, and centuries later they invited corruption back in to keep themselves above it. The fall they tried to escape arrived through their own desperate choice.\n\n═══ YUÈLÌ'S FALL AND THE SUNDERING LAND ═══\n\nIn the deep mythic past, the mother of chaos — **Yuèlì** — descended into madness. The corruption that came with her broke loose from her body and began to spread through the world below. The land itself sundered. Forests darkened. Rivers turned. The continent of Avalúne began to suffer a wound it could not close.\n\nThis was the world the ancestors of Aurum lived in — a land that was beginning to rot from below. They watched the corruption move through soil and water and beast, and they understood, with the cold clarity that comes to people who have already lost too much, that staying meant being consumed.\n\n═══ THE ANCESTORS' FLIGHT UPWARD ═══\n\n**Serina's verbatim:** *\"Aurum's Ansectors felt that to escape the calamity that was sundering the land at the time the fall of Yueli that uprooting Aurum and taking to the skys would protect them.\"*\n\nThey chose flight — not in ships, not in horses, but in LAND. Using primordial cosmic-tier magic, the ancestors **tore pieces of the continent from their roots and lifted them into the sky.** Real land. Original geology. Native ecosystems. The Dìwǔ paulownia at the centre of a vast subterranean lake, lifted with everything around it. Forests, fields, hills, springs, animals, ancestral villages — all of it, taken upward together.\n\nThey did not try to lift everything. They lifted enough — a constellation of land-pieces sufficient to carry their people, their culture, their crops, their gods'-gift tree. Each piece they lifted took a portion of the underground waterways with it (see `aurum-underrock` for the water-cycle canon). What they left behind was the rotting continent, the corruption-bleeding land, and the world below the clouds that they would teach their children to forget.\n\nThe LIFT was a one-time act. A magic so vast it has not been repeated, possibly cannot be repeated. The ancestors wove enough lifting-spell into each island to keep it aloft on the innate magic of the land itself plus the underground water it carried up. Then they sealed the work and called it done.\n\n═══ THE MAGIC-BAN AS PROTECTION ═══\n\n**Serina's verbatim:** *\"To make sure that they would never fall prey to the curruption spreading through the land they decided once the Islands where up and floating they would ban magic.\"*\n\nThe ancestors understood that the corruption spreading below was a CORRUPTION OF MAGIC. The cure they chose was extreme: they outlawed magic itself in the new sky cities. No cultivation. No spell-craft. No magical practice of any kind. The reasoning was simple — if magic was the door corruption walked through, they would close the door entirely.\n\nThis was not a religious ban. This was a SAFETY ban. The founders' generation had watched what corruption did. They wanted their grandchildren and their grandchildren's grandchildren to never see what they had seen. The ban was passed into law, into custom, into education, into every layer of Aurum life. Magic became the great forbidden thing — taught about only as a danger to be avoided.\n\nThe original lifting magic was grandfathered in — it was already done, already woven, already holding the islands up. Nobody used new magic. The old magic in the lifted land and the underground water was enough.\n\n═══ THE CENTURIES IT WORKED ═══\n\n**Serina's verbatim:** *\"for a time this worked the inate magic in the lands iteself and the underground water ways was able to keep the islands afloat.\"*\n\nFor centuries — possibly for over a thousand years — Aurum was almost innocent. The islands floated on their own ancient magic. The people built their pagoda neighbourhoods, raised their children, made their art, traded carefully with the surface, and lived above the rotting world without ever contributing to its rot. Magic was unknown but not missed. The Dìwǔ tree at the heart of the Royal Island stood quiet and uncalled-upon, its corruption-layer not yet awakened.\n\nThis was Aurum's golden age — a real one, not the gilded mask the realm wears now. The ancestors' covenant was holding. The escape had worked. The children of the ancestors grew up never knowing the corruption their grandparents had fled, taught only that the surface was sundered and dangerous and best forgotten. The realm was safe.\n\nThis is the Aurum that the realm still tells stories about. The pagodas, the waterfalls, the airships drifting between islands at sunset. The merchant trade growing rich. The Royal House gracious and beloved. The Underrock not yet a caste — just the people who tended the engines below, equal in dignity if not in altitude.\n\nFor a while.\n\n═══ THE ORIGINAL MAGIC FADES ═══\n\nMagic, even ancient magic woven by primordial powers, is not infinite. The lifting magic was a one-time wound in the cosmic order; it healed slowly over centuries the way every wound does. The underground waterways the islands carried began to thin. The innate magic in the lifted soil began to dry. The Dìwǔ tree's quiet contribution was no longer enough to compensate.\n\n**The original magic dried out. Gone. Aurum's central historical wound — the gift has run dry.**\n\nNobody noticed at first. The islands held steady. Then a small island slipped — only a few feet, then a few more. Then a dozen. Then a hundred. Then several. The Royal House watched the elevation readings with growing alarm. The merchants began to notice that some routes were lower than they had been a generation ago. The Underrock workers reported strange murmurs from the engine cores.\n\n═══ THE ISLANDS BEGIN TO DESCEND ═══\n\n**Serina's verbatim:** *\"over centuries the magic started to fade the islands started to decend the upper nobles and the royals needed a way to fix this.\"*\n\nThe descent was slow but undeniable. Generation by generation, the islands sank a little closer to the corrupted continent below. The cloud layer that had hidden the surface was getting thinner. From the lowest islands, on a clear day, you could occasionally GLIMPSE the rotting land — a green that was not green, a fog that did not behave like fog. The Royal House quietly forbade looking down.\n\nDesperation entered the throne room. The ancestors' covenant had assumed the original magic would hold forever. It had not. The realm faced a choice no one had prepared for: continue descending until the islands met the corruption their ancestors had fled — or find another way to stay aloft.\n\nThe Royal House could not turn to magic — magic was outlawed. They could not turn to the surface — the surface was the rot they had escaped. They could not lift the islands again — the original magic that had done so was unrepeatable. Engineering could stabilise; it could not levitate. Every door they tried opened onto the same wall.\n\nAnd then a door opened that nobody had built. Or rather — a door was opened FOR them, by hands they did not know belonged to the very corruption they had spent centuries fleeing.\n\n═══ THE CRISIS THAT OPENED THE DOOR FOR THE SHI SECT ═══\n\nInto a court that was running out of options walked **Guì the Blood Witch.** She came in the robes of a learned outsider — a quiet, cultivated woman from across the sea, a scholar of old magics who had heard of Aurum's troubles and offered her counsel. The Royal House, who had been raised never to use magic, listened nonetheless because there was nowhere else to listen.\n\nGuì proposed the unthinkable. There was a way. Not magic the way the ancestors had practised it — something quieter, controllable, *limited.* A small, ritualised use of life-force, channelled through the Dìwǔ tree, would arrest the descent. Only a small amount, she promised. Only the willing. Only when needed. The ancestors' magic-ban could be preserved publicly; the ritual would be kept in the highest counsel and never spoken of beyond the throne.\n\nThe Royal House — terrified of the falling islands, desperate to save the realm, isolated from any other source of advice — agreed. The first ritual was small. The descent slowed. The relief was overwhelming. The next ritual was a little larger, a little less willing, a little more efficient. The descent halted. Within a generation, Aurum was steady in the sky again.\n\nThe Royal House did not know what they had welcomed. They thought they had hired a clever outsider with a useful technique. They had actually opened the door to the **Shi Sect** — Yuèlì's pre-fall network, the very corruption their ancestors had fled the land to escape. (See `aurum-bloodmagic` for what the technique actually was, and `aurum-royalhouse` for who Guì really is and how she has held the post for centuries by faking her death every fifty years.)\n\nThe central irony of Aurum's history is now complete. The corruption their ancestors escaped by tearing land into the sky did not die. It came back. It walked through the throne room door wearing a scholar's robes and offering a solution. The Royal House thanked it and gave it a chair. It has never left.\n\nAurum's history is the history of a people who tried to outrun their wound and ended up feeding it from above.
