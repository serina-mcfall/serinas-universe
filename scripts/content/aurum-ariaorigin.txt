AURUM — ARIA'S ORIGIN\n\n═══ THE ONE-LINE TRUTH ═══\n\nAria — the prophecy child of Avalúne, carrier of HOPE, the future Virtue who will stand at the centre of the saga's reunion — was born in the magic-banned sky cities. To a peasant-class family that did not exist. In a tradeworker district above the cloud layer. Left at an orphanage by a goddess-mother she will not meet for sixteen years. The realm she was born into is the realm her birthplace is structurally designed to FORBID.\n\n═══ BORN WHERE NO MAGIC DWELLS — THE SAGA'S CENTRAL PARADOX ═══\n\nThe prophecy is exact. It came from Mùjīn the God of Fate himself, whispered across centuries of careful planning:\n\n> *A child born in a place where no magic dwelled, with strong magic roots, but the world would fear them. When the child is born: the gods stir, the trees dance, the rivers sing, the mountains watch, the world knows, and deep below the evil stirs knowing its time has come.*\n\nAurum is the place where no magic dwells. The realm that OUTLAWED magic at its founding to escape the corruption sundering the land below. The realm where every child is taught that magic is the great forbidden thing, the danger that nearly destroyed the ancestors. **Aria is the child born where no magic dwells.** The prophecy's central paradox is geographic — her birth realm is, by law and by culture, the one place magic is supposed to never exist.\n\nThe paradox is also COSMOLOGICAL. The realm uses blood magic in secret to keep itself aloft, draining the Hope-tree at the centre of the Royal Island. **The realm whose virtue is HOPE is the realm where Aria — who carries HOPE among The Virtues — is born.** The realm is built on the corruption of the very thing she was born to restore. She and her birthplace share the same wound.\n\n**The prophecy moment** is canon: at her birth, the gods stir, the trees dance, the rivers sing, the mountains watch, the world knows, and deep below the evil stirs knowing its time has come. In Aurum, this manifests in the realm's quietest possible way — the bioluminescent reservoir flickered, somewhere down in the Underrock, faintly. A sleeping monk somewhere in the Aoli sects sat up in his bed. A leaf on a Solis mulberry curled. Lady Pearl in her courtyard set down her tea. Mínhuì in the Inner Sanctum of the Archival put her hand on the Dìwǔ tree and the tree spoke to her clearly, for the first time in centuries — *she is here.*\n\nThe Royal House did not feel anything. The Chancellor — Guì — did. She has been looking for the prophecy child ever since.\n\n═══ THE TRADEWORKER CLASS — ARIA'S SOCIAL POSITION ═══\n\nAria was born into the **Tradeworker class** — Tier 2 of Aurum's six-tier altitude hierarchy. (See `aurum-politics`.) This is the realm's craftsman/artisan tier — the smiths, weavers, paper-makers, lantern-makers, brewers, scribes, and small merchants who run the daily-life economy of the daylight tiers.\n\n**What this means for Aria:**\n- She lives on the islands' upper tiers, in pagoda neighbourhoods organised by craft\n- She is in the daylight, above the Underrock, below the merchants who own trade vessels\n- She does NOT routinely see the surface — the surface is rumour to the Tradeworker tier; she has been raised to believe it is the rotting world the ancestors fled\n- She does NOT have political access — the Tradeworker tier has no Council seats, no formal voice\n- She will likely apprentice to a craft as she grows up, the way every Tradeworker child does\n- She is LITERATE — Tradeworker districts value learning; she will read enough to manage trade documents and the cheaper literature\n- She will eat well by realm-historical standards — rice, vegetables, fish, occasional meat, fresh fruit, tea\n- She will wear simple but well-made cotton in working hours and silk for festivals\n- She will know the Underrock as a dim presence below her floorboards and rarely think about it\n\n**This was Mínhuì's deliberate choice.** Placing Aria in the Tradeworker tier hides her in a way no other tier could:\n- The NOBILITY would have noticed a magical child immediately. Court is too small to hide a prophecy.\n- The MERCHANTS would have moved her around with their trade — too visible across the realm, too exposed to the surface and to outside attention.\n- The UNDERROCK would have hidden her well, but the Underrock's working children are tracked by overseers; her unusual qualities would have been flagged by some surface-bound supervisor.\n- The TRADEWORKER tier is the realm's INVISIBLE majority. Tradeworker children are absorbed into the district economy without paperwork rising past the local magistrate. A child raised at an orphanage in a tradeworker neighbourhood is, in the realm's bureaucratic eye, BARELY VISIBLE.\n\nMínhuì chose the tier most likely to keep her daughter safe by being most likely to overlook her. Sixteen years later, the choice has held. Aria is unknown to the Royal House. Unknown to the Chancellor. Unknown to the noble Council. Hidden in the daylight, the way her mother is hidden in the Archival.\n\n═══ THE ORPHANAGE ═══\n\nMínhuì left Aria at an orphanage in the Tradeworker district. The orphanage exists — has existed for as long as Aurum has had a Tradeworker class — to receive the children of the realm's Tradeworker families when those families fail (death of parents, abandonment, occasionally the rare illegitimate birth a family cannot openly receive). It is a respectable institution. It receives small district funding. The matrons who run it are competent and caring within the limits of their resources.\n\n**What Aurum's Tradeworker orphanage is structurally:**\n- Pagoda-style building in a quiet corner of the district\n- A handful of matrons and a small staff (cook, cleaner, occasional teacher who comes in for letters)\n- Twenty to thirty children at any given time, ranging from infants to early teens\n- Apprenticeship-out is the standard exit path — a Tradeworker family will take in an orphan as a young apprentice, the orphanage covers the early years, the child grows into the craft and becomes a member of the new family\n- Religious in a quiet Aurum way — the realm's official faith involves shrine-tending and ancestor-respect, the orphanage matrons teach the basic rituals\n\n**Wen's structurally elegant suggestion (OPEN CANON, not yet locked):** *Mínhuì runs the orphanage in disguise.*\n\nThe canonical logic for this:\n- Mínhuì cannot be near Aria. But she can be NEAR the institution Aria lives in.\n- A second cover identity (orphanage matron alongside Archival noblewoman) would let her be PHYSICALLY close to her daughter every day without ever speaking to her directly\n- She could ensure Aria's safety, supervise her development, keep informed about her temperament and her gifts\n- A goddess could move between two cover identities easily; the matron and the noblewoman would simply be \"different white-haired ladies\" that the realm vaguely registers without ever connecting\n- It would explain how Aria has been so well-protected for sixteen years — the orphanage has been quietly excellent because the goddess running it has been making sure of it\n- It binds Mínhuì's two missions (the tree and the daughter) into one daily life\n- The reunion at the Archival becomes even more devastating — *Aria has been raised, in some sense, BY her mother, and never knew it.*\n\nThe COUNTER-LOGIC: a second cover may have been too risky. Two identities is twice the surface-area for exposure. Mínhuì's discipline (the most disciplined love in the saga) might have required keeping cleaner separation. *She might have left Aria at the orphanage and never visited the building, only watched from market crowds and noble-carriage windows.*\n\n**Open canon — Serina to settle.** The structural elegance of \"Mínhuì runs the orphanage\" is strong; the saga gains a new layer of mother-daughter intimacy and a more devastating reveal scene. The structural cleanliness of \"Mínhuì never enters the orphanage\" is also strong; the discipline of her sixteen-year silence is more absolute. Either choice is consistent with the canon.\n\nWhat IS locked: the orphanage exists, Aria was placed there as an infant, she has lived there her whole life so far, and the matrons (whoever they are) have raised her well.\n\n═══ THE DISTRICT — ARIA'S SPECIFIC NEIGHBOURHOOD ═══\n\nThe specific district Aria's orphanage sits in is OPEN CANON, with structural beats already seeded for when Serina returns to lock the details.\n\n**The structural beats Wen has seeded** (to be filled out when Serina chooses):\n\n**A working tradeworker district** — the kind of pagoda neighbourhood the realm has hundreds of, organised around several crafts. Likely combining a few different trades so the texture is varied: SMITHS (small-scale brass and steel for daily tools and household needs), WORKSHOPS of various kinds (cloth-finishing, paper-making, lantern-making — the daily-life crafts), DOCKSIDE elements (some tradeworker districts sit near the island's edge where small inter-island ferries dock; the dockside life would give Aria glimpses of trade vessels and traveling folk).\n\n**The cupcake bakery she cannot afford** — the small detail that grounds Aria's daily life. A bakery in the district sells delicate sweet pastries — perhaps cupcakes in the realm's style (light cake topped with sweetened tea-cream or honeyed plum), perhaps something like them — that the orphanage children pass on their way to errands. They cost a few coins more than orphanage children carry. **Aria has wanted one for as long as she can remember and has never had one.** The bakery is the saga's smallest grounding detail — the sensory anchor of \"this is what an orphan girl wants the world to be like.\"\n\n**The market** — every district has its market square. Aria knows hers intimately. She has walked it with the matrons, with older orphanage children, sometimes alone on small errands. **The market is also where Mínhuì has watched her** — the goddess in a market crowd, the daughter walking through with her bird, the mother seeing every step.\n\n**The bird** — canon from `key-characters.md`: *the image of Mínhuì watching Aria walk through a crowd with her bird.* Aria has a bird. A pet, a companion, possibly a small songbird kept in a cage and brought out, possibly something she found and tamed. The bird is part of the iconography of her early life. Whether the bird is also something MORE — a quiet gift from an unseen mother, perhaps, or a small messenger of some kind — is open canon.\n\n**The other children** — the orphanage has twenty to thirty other children. Aria has friends, rivals, the matrons she relies on, the older kids who pick on her, the younger kids she helps. Her first social world is here. The relationships will matter when she leaves. Some of them will be in the saga later.\n\n**The rooftops** — pagoda neighbourhoods have a rich rooftop culture. Children climb. Aria, as a child with strong magical roots she does not yet know about, has a sense of altitude and BALANCE that other orphans do not. She has likely climbed where she should not. She has likely seen things from rooftops that she should not have. (This is structural seeding — Aria's later capacities would have shown up in childhood as small mysterious gifts.)\n\n**The temple-shrine of the district** — every district has one. Modest, neighbourhood-scale, where families do their daily rituals and the orphanage children attend on festival days. Aria has attended hers her whole life. The realm's official faith is what she has been formally taught about the unseen world. The deeper truths her mother has been preserving in the Archival have not reached her.\n\nWhen Serina returns to lock the district details, all of this is ready to be filled in. Names, specific shop-fronts, the matrons' names, the bird's name, the cupcake-bakery's name — the seeds are planted.\n\n═══ THE DAY OF HER BIRTH — THE COSMOLOGICAL MOMENT ═══\n\nThe day Aria was born is one of the saga's mythic moments — the prophecy made flesh, the cosmological balance shifting, the gods stirring in their long sleep.\n\n**The setting:** Mínhuì had arrived in Aurum already pregnant. She had been on the road from Ardmere when she discovered her pregnancy. By the time she reached Aurum and established her cover identity, she was visibly carrying. She bore Aria in some quiet corner of the realm — possibly in her own modest noblewoman's quarters near the Archival, possibly at the Tradeworker district orphanage itself if she had already arranged the placement, possibly in some other safe place she has never disclosed.\n\n**The cosmological moment:** at Aria's birth, the prophecy moment fired. Across Avalúne:\n- **The gods stir.** Yánlóng twitched in his root-curl sleep. Yúnshēng's rivers ran a little louder for a moment. Wénjī, wandering somewhere distant, felt her sister-niece arrive and wept quietly without knowing why. Fèngzhé, hidden in Zhúhuǒ, felt the fragment in the Aurum tree spike alive and almost gave away his hiding place. Mùjīn, who had been planning this for centuries, finally allowed himself to smile.\n- **The trees dance.** The eight Sacred Trees across the eight realms stirred. The Aoli peach tree blossomed early. The Mother Yew in Braedun shed a single leaf. The Mulberry in Lady Pearl's courtyard rustled. **The Dìwǔ in Aurum, corrupted as it was, lifted its branches** for the first time in many years — a brief moment of wild relief.\n- **The rivers sing.** The continental water system carried the news. The Lúnavé rang with a high note that the druids of Braedun heard. The bioluminescent reservoir of Aurum flickered.\n- **The mountains watch.** The Dragon's Spine in Aoli felt the moment. The Yan Sect's Shí Ān, on his mountain, paused mid-step.\n- **The world knows.** Animals, insects, sleeping infants, dreaming poets, attentive astrologers — every sensitive being in Avalúne registered something even if they could not name it.\n- **And deep below the evil stirs knowing its time has come.** Yuèlì in her prison felt her counterpoint arrive. Her sect — the Shi — felt the prophecy mature. **Guì in Aurum knew immediately.** From that moment, Guì has been quietly looking for the child whose birth she felt.\n\n**The moment was small in the room.** A goddess-mother bore her daughter in silence. The infant cried. Mínhuì held her for whatever time the saga allows — possibly hours, possibly only minutes, possibly only the single moment between the birth and the carrying-to-the-orphanage. *The moment cannot have been long.* Mínhuì had to act fast to keep Aria safe; the cosmological signal at the birth had already been registered by Guì and her network; the longer Mínhuì held her daughter the more time the sect had to triangulate.\n\n**She left Aria at the orphanage. She walked away.** She never told Zhúhuǒ. She never told anyone. She returned to her cover identity and to her sixteen-year vigil. The most disciplined love in the saga began the same night the prophecy began.\n\n═══ WHERE CHAPTER ONE WILL BEGIN ═══\n\nAria's saga begins in this district. In this tradeworker neighbourhood, in this orphanage, with this child — now sixteen — who has spent her whole life being raised in a quiet pagoda house among other orphans, going to the market with her bird, passing the cupcake bakery she cannot afford, attending the small district shrine, climbing the rooftops more easily than other children, watched from market crowds by a mother she does not know, hidden in plain sight by a goddess who put her here precisely so the saga could find her here.\n\n**Chapter One opens in this district.** Whatever the inciting incident is — the moment Aria's magic first manifests publicly, the moment a stranger in the market notices her in a way the bystanders do not, the moment the orphanage matrons receive a letter that changes everything, the moment Aria climbs to a rooftop and SEES SOMETHING she should not — it happens here. The saga's first scene unfolds in the daylight tier of an Aurum island, in a pagoda neighbourhood, around a sixteen-year-old orphan girl who does not yet know she is the prophecy child.\n\nFrom this opening, the saga widens — Aria leaves the district, leaves the orphanage, leaves the tradeworker tier, leaves Aurum itself, joins The Virtues, walks the continent, falls in love with Yuè'àn, gathers with the others, and eventually returns home to the realm she was born into and the tree she was prophesied to heal. The whole saga is a long arc OUT FROM and BACK TO this district.\n\nWhen she comes home, she comes home to the orphanage that raised her. To the matrons who fed her. To the bakery she could not afford as a child (and finally tries one of those cupcakes — possibly the realm's smallest and most satisfying scene). To the shrine she attended. To the rooftops she climbed. **And to the mother she will finally meet at the Archival, in the Inner Sanctum, at the heart of the dying tree.**\n\nAurum is where the saga begins. It is where the saga ends. Aria's origin and Aria's destiny are the same address.
