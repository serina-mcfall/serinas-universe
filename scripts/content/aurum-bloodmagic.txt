AURUM — THE BLOOD MAGIC SECRET\n\n═══ THE ONE-LINE TRUTH ═══\n\nAurum's gilded sky cities float on stolen life. Continuous lives are required to keep them aloft. Magic is publicly outlawed precisely so no commoner asks where the levitation comes from — and the Dìwǔ Hope-tree at the realm's heart is being drained as a battery to pump the blood through the engines.\n\n═══ WHAT IT REPLACED ═══\n\nFor centuries, Aurum needed nothing to stay in the sky. The original lifting magic — woven by the ancestors when they tore the land from the rotting continent — held the islands aloft on the innate magic of the lifted soil and the underground waterways the islands had carried up. The Dìwǔ tree's quiet contribution kept the system topped up. The covenant was self-sustaining. (See `aurum-history`.)\n\nWhen the original magic faded and the islands began to descend, that self-sustaining system FAILED. Aurum needed a substitute — a power source strong enough to keep the islands aloft, regular enough to be reliable, and discreet enough to be hidden from the public.\n\nBlood magic was the substitute. The Royal House did not invent it; **Guì the Blood Witch** brought it to them in their hour of crisis (see `aurum-royalhouse`). What had once been a willing-offering covenant — devotion freely given to a sacred tree — became a STOLEN-LIFE engine. The shortcut became method. The tree became addicted to wrong food.\n\nThe core difference is the one Aurum will never publicly acknowledge:\n- The ancestors' covenant: life *offered* to the tree by people who knew what they were doing\n- Aurum's blood magic: life *taken* from people who do not know it is being taken at all\n\n═══ HOW IT WORKS ═══\n\nThe mechanics, as preserved in canon:\n\n**The principle.** Living beings carry life-force. Blood is the densest and most accessible form of that life-force. When blood is drawn under the right conditions and channelled into the right system, the life-force can be EXTRACTED from the blood and redirected into other magical work — including the levitation work that keeps the islands aloft.\n\n**The channel.** The life-force does not power the levitation directly. It is channelled INTO THE DÌWǓ TREE. The tree absorbs the stolen life and releases it back out as the lifting energy that keeps the islands in the sky. The tree functions as a magical capacitor, a battery, a transformer — taking raw stolen life and converting it into the specific kind of magic the islands need to float.\n\n**The continuity.** Levitation is not a one-time charge. It is an ONGOING DRAW. The islands need the energy continuously, every hour of every day. Which means the blood magic must be FED continuously. Stop the feed, and the islands begin to descend within weeks. Stop it for long enough, and they fall.\n\n**The scale.** A small noble's life-force charged into the tree might keep one island steady for a day. The system requires far more than the willing-noble model could ever provide. It requires industrial input. It requires a HIDDEN POPULATION whose life-force can be drawn on regularly without raising public alarm.\n\nThat hidden population works in the engines beneath the islands. (See `aurum-underrock`.)\n\n═══ THE TREE AS BATTERY ═══\n\nThe **Dìwǔ paulownia** is the system's heart. The Royal Island grew around the tree because the tree is the centre of the original subterranean lake the ancestors lifted; the Royal House lives ON the Hope-tree's heart because that is the only place the throne can be while the throne is the executor of the tree's exploitation.\n\nFour layers of the tree's nature compose what Aurum is doing to it:\n\n1. **Original purpose — Tree of Reincarnation / willing sacrifice.** Planted to be fed by offered devotion. Aurum perverted this layer.\n2. **Corruption — Dìwǔ / blood magic.** Aurum addicted the tree to stolen life faster than it could be offered. The shortcut became method. The tree no longer remembers how to receive offered devotion the way it once did — its uptake mechanism has been remade for blood.\n3. **Consciousness — Primordial Sacred Tree.** The tree is ALIVE and AWARE. It knows. It grieves. It tries to speak but the blood clouds its voice. Only **Mínhuì** can hear clearly. (See `aurum-minhui`.)\n4. **Phoenix fragment — Fèngzhé's essence.** The tree is the phoenix god's sacred roosting place. He left a fragment of himself in it before he was forced to flee. The blood-magic feed is also draining at the fragment.\n\nThe Hope-virtue the tree was planted to guard is being CORRUPTED by the very system that keeps Aurum aloft. Every day the realm stays in the sky, the virtue it was given to protect bleeds a little more. *\"Hope is traded for bread.\"*\n\n═══ WHO FEEDS IT — ENGINE STOKERS, LIVES ═══\n\nThe blood magic that holds the realm in the sky has to come from SOMEWHERE. It comes from the Underrock.\n\n**Engine stokers** — the lowest-tier Underrock workers — feed the engines on every island. Their job, as taught above the clouds, is to keep the boilers running, the pipes pressurised, the brass mechanisms lubricated. This is partly true. The engines do exist; the steam machinery is real. But woven through the engineering, in chambers most stokers will never see, is the blood-magic apparatus. And the stokers — slowly, unknowingly, across long working lives — give up the life-force that the apparatus draws from.\n\nThe drawing is not visible. Stokers do not bleed. They do not weaken visibly day by day. They do not see vessels carrying their blood away. The drain is woven into the air they breathe down there, the water they drink from the bioluminescent reservoir, the work itself — every shift in the engine room is a slow contribution to the levitation pulse. **The stokers are unknowing batteries.** They die earlier than upper-tier folk. They look older at fifty than a tradeworker looks at seventy. They cough. Their lungs darken. Their hair greys early. They are told this is the price of working with steam.\n\n**Body-keepers** — a specific Underrock job — handle the dead. When a stoker passes, the body-keepers take the corpse to a chamber the upper class never sees. The remaining life-force is harvested in a final ritual. Then the body is given the rites and returned. Only the body-keepers know what they do. They do not speak of it. (Some of them know. Most of them only suspect.)\n\nThe upper class knows that the engines are powered by *something*. Some upper nobles have heard the term \"blood magic\" whispered. Some choose not to ask. Some ask and are quietly told to stop asking. The Royal House knows the whole system. The High Chancellor — Guì — runs it.\n\nThe lives required are continuous, ongoing, generational. **Aurum's centuries of stability since the descent began have cost an unbroken chain of Underrock lives.** Every island the merchant class admires from a sky deck has been kept aloft on the slow death of the people working in the rock beneath it.\n\n═══ WHY IT'S OUTLAWED PUBLICLY ═══\n\nMagic is illegal in Aurum. The ancestors' ban from the time of the Uplift was originally a SAFETY measure — keep the corruption out by closing the door magic walks through. After Guì's arrival and the introduction of blood magic, the ban took on a SECOND function the ancestors never intended:\n\n**The magic-ban now exists to hide the blood magic.**\n\nIf magic is illegal, no commoner studies it. If no commoner studies it, no commoner asks where the levitation comes from — they are taught to assume it is the ancestors' ancient gift, still working as it always has. The system can run for centuries because nobody knowledgeable enough to ask the right questions is allowed to ask any question at all.\n\nThe ban is enforced strictly in the upper tiers. A commoner caught practising any kind of magic faces severe punishment. The Royal House delivers public sentences with appropriate gravity, and every such sentence reinforces in the public mind that *magic is the great evil — only the rituals administered through the proper channels keep the realm safe.* The public never connects the dots.\n\nThe Underrock is policed differently. The tier is so dismissed that whatever folk-magic flickers down there in the dark — small wards, bioluminescent reservoir-blessings, quiet old practices the ancestors brought up with the land — is largely ignored. As long as it does not threaten the engines, nobody bothers to send guards down to enforce. The Underrock is its own world; the upper tiers prefer to keep it that way.\n\n**This is also why ARIA was such a threat.** A child born with magic, in the magic-banned sky cities, in the Tradeworker class — visible, in the daylight tiers, where she could not be overlooked. The prophecy child arrived in the worst possible place for a Royal House desperate to keep its central lie intact. (See `aurum-ariaorigin`.)\n\n═══ THE COST ═══\n\n**The cost to the Underrock:** lives. Generations of them. Slow drain disguised as a working life. Early death disguised as the price of steam. The lowest tier of Aurum has been a magical fuel-source for centuries, and most of them die without ever knowing what they actually did with their working years.\n\n**The cost to the Dìwǔ tree:** corruption. The Hope-virtue it was planted to guard is being bled out alongside the life-force it transmutes. Mínhuì has watched this for centuries and can only hold back what she can. (See `aurum-minhui`.) The day the tree finally dies is the day Aurum falls from the sky — and the day HOPE goes out of the world entirely until restored.\n\n**The cost to the Royal House:** complicity. They were CONNED into starting the system (they thought Guì was a useful outsider with a clever technique). Centuries later they are deep enough in that they cannot stop without watching the realm fall. Every generation of royals has inherited the secret and made the same calculation. Hundreds of thousands of sky-folk lives versus an unknown number of Underrock lives over time. They keep choosing the former. They have become cruel through fear.\n\n**The cost to AURUM AS A REALM:** moral. The realm's beauty, its pagoda art, its merchant prosperity, its golden sunsets — all of it sits on a foundation the realm cannot acknowledge. The most beautiful place in Avalúne is one of the most corrupted. The realm whose virtue is HOPE is being drained of the very thing it was made to protect.\n\n**The cost the saga turns on:** the day Aria touches the tree. The fragment of Fèngzhé in the tree will recognise her blood. The corruption will rage. Her divine bloodline will answer the fragment like a flame answers a candle. **This may be the moment Aria understands what she actually is** — not just prophecy child, not just builder, but the carrier of the phoenix's returning fire. Her body is the home her father has been keeping for her uncle. And the system that has powered Aurum for centuries on stolen life has met the one being whose blood it cannot drain — because she is what the tree was originally PLANTED to receive.
