THIRD PRINCE CHÉN QĪNG (辰清) — \"CLEAR STAR\"\nThird and youngest son of Emperor Chén Yuán and Empress Lí Wǎn. Younger brother to Crown Prince Chén Jǐng and Second Prince Chén Wǔ. Brother to Princess Chén Lán.\n\nConcealed at Hun Sect as **YŌU LÁN — \"THE DREAM WALKER,\"** the isolated enigma of the Dream Walking discipline. The son behind the veil of mist.\n\n═══ THE OPEN SECRET ═══\n\nChén Qīng is the Chén prince who walked away from the imperial architecture entirely.\n\nHe refused the whole frame his parents had given him — the prince's title, the scholar's path, the soldier's path, the official's path. Wǔ chose the Guard. Jǐng IS the throne. Lán serves at court as Minister of Music. **Only Qīng walked away.** He left the palace, took up cultivation, entered Hun Sect, and became something else: Yōu Lán the Dream Walker, a quiet cultivator of a quieter discipline, isolated in the far reaches of Hun's valley of mist.\n\n**The court does not know.** Yōu Lán the Dream Walker remains the buried plot gem. When the Fellowship eventually visits the Hun Sect, the reveal is available to them.\n\nSerina's verbatim, from Lí Wǎn Q6: *\"She grieves this son for he choose not officialdom, to be a scholor or a warrior he choose to become a member of a sect. He choose to leave behind what she and his father had given him his title of prince and become something else. He rarely comes home, he does write home occationlly when he has news or needs to tell her something. He has effectivly become her eyes and ears in the sects for he knows what is happening within. She does not resent his choice but she still grieves the loss of her youngest son.\"*\n\n═══ MOTHER KNOWS, FATHER SUSPECTS ═══\n\n- **Lí Wǎn KNOWS** her son is Yōu Lán. She has known from early on. He writes to her when he has news or needs to tell her something. She grieves him without resenting him — both true at once, the hardest grief to hold.\n- **Chén Yuán SUSPECTS.** He does not formally know. He is no fool; he has put pieces together. But he has chosen not to interrogate it. Another silence in the marriage.\n- **The court does not know.** Jǐng likely knows — he is the Crown Prince, he has his own intelligence habits, and the brothers' bonds run deeper than court rumour. Wǔ likely knows through the Guard's quiet channels. Lán's knowledge is open canon — she is closest to their mother and may have inherited the secret through her.\n- **The Mù do not know.** If they did, they would weaponise it immediately — a Chén prince inside a sect is a violation of the Mandate-of-Heaven two-pillar separation, and the Mù would use it to corner the Empress.\n\nThis is **a secret channel across the two pillars.** In Aoli's Zhou-coded cosmology, the Emperor rules settled lands under Heaven's mandate; the sects hold their own mandate from the sleeping gods. The two pillars are meant to be separate. Qīng's concealment inside Hun Sect, combined with his mother receiving intelligence through him, is a structural violation dressed as a young cultivator's solitary practice.\n\n═══ HER EYES AND EARS IN THE SECTS ═══\n\n**STRUCTURAL CANON — He is the Empress's intelligence channel into the sect world.**\n\nThrough Qīng, Lí Wǎn has private intelligence on what is happening inside Hun Sect — and likely fragmentary knowledge of the other five sects through Hun's network of spirit-work, veil-walking, and dream-travel. The Empress of Aoli has a secret channel into the sect world that the Mandate formally forbids.\n\nIf the Mù discovered it, they would weaponise it. If the Lí discovered it, they would claim it. Even Chén Yuán might be uneasy with how intimate this channel is. It is one of the silences in their marriage.\n\n**Qīng has not fully renounced the throne.** He still serves it — but through one specific person: **his mother. Not his father. HER.** That distinction is enormous. He left the throne's architecture and rebuilt his own service inside it. He is the youngest son doing quiet work no one asked him to do, for the mother who did not ask him to do it.\n\nThis makes his mother's intelligence picture one of the densest in the empire. Jǐng has Lìyǐn's southern network. Wǔ may have the Imperial Guard. **Lí Wǎn has Qīng inside the sects — a third channel, unknown to either son.** Three Chén-family intelligence streams feeding into one matriarch, none of them knowing about the other two.\n\n═══ HIS DOMAIN — HUN SECT DREAM WALKING ═══\n\nHun Sect (魂织 — Soul Weave) is the all-cultivation sect of spirit and the veil between life and death. Founded by Mínhuì, though Mínhuì is now trapped beyond the veil, her body preserved in the sacred cave. Five disciplines, each with an eye-colour price paid for its practice.\n\n**DREAM WALKING** is the isolated enigma of the five. Qīng as Yōu Lán walks dreams nobody else will ever see. He travels where other cultivators cannot. He is solitary in his discipline — Dream Walking requires a particular kind of inward geography, and it cannot be taught through company.\n\nHe has not taken disciples in the recent intake. The mantle of Dream Walking is his alone at the moment. The Dream Walker continues his cultivation in the far reaches of the valley of mist, walking dreams at night, reporting dreams to his mother by letter.\n\n═══ HIS ISOLATION ═══\n\nHe rarely comes home. Distance is real and ongoing. The physical return to the palace is rare; the emotional return via letter is regular. **Functional letters, not pure social.** His mother is mothering an intelligence asset as well as her youngest son.\n\nThe isolation is not loneliness alone — it is also protection. The further Yōu Lán is from the palace, the harder it is for anyone to realise he is Chén Qīng. The mist of Hun Sect's valley is the literal camouflage around the identity he chose.\n\n═══ CANONICAL QUIETUDE — THE LETTERS ═══\n\n**Qīng speaks to his family through letters.**\n\n- **To his mother Lí Wǎn** — regular, functional, carrying news and intelligence. She reads them alone in her study, by the single lamp, after the Emperor has gone to his side. She does not speak of them openly. *(The prayer beads pass through her fingers as she reads.)*\n- **To his brother Jǐng** — if the brothers write, their letters are likely quiet and infrequent, the bond between them the steady background of the Chén line. Jǐng has Lìyǐn's letters across the Dragon Spine; Qīng has Jǐng's letters across the mist. Two Chén brothers maintaining their bonds through ink.\n- **To his sister Lán** — possibly the most emotionally open letters. Lán is the closest child to their mother, the one who senses the unspoken weights. Qīng may write to her most when he needs someone to listen.\n- **To his father Chén Yuán** — rarely, if ever. Qīng walked away from the frame his father built as much as from the frame his mother shared in building. The silence between them is the unanswered question of his choice.\n\n═══ THE PALACE HE WAS BORN IN ═══\n\nSerina's verbatim (from the closing of `aoli-imperial`): *\"And somewhere in the Hun Sect's valley of mist, a figure called Yōu Lán — 'The Dream Walker,' the isolated enigma — will step out of his quiet life and remember the palace he was born in.\"*\n\nWhen the saga's architecture calls for it — when the Mù overreach, when the Mandate cracks, when the Fellowship reaches Aoli, when the truth of Chén Míng's murder finally surfaces, when his sister needs him or his mother calls him home — Yōu Lán will step out of the mist and become Chén Qīng again.\n\nHe does not know that his brother Jǐng is running a parallel investigation through a continental spy network. He does not know that his brother Wǔ may be running a parallel investigation inside the palace itself. He does not know that his sister Lán was named for the orchid-grief that hangs over their whole generation. What he knows is the shape of his own dreams, the letters he owes his mother, and the fact that the palace he left is still waiting for the day he comes back.\n\n═══ OPEN CANON ═══\n\n- **How old is Qīng** in saga-time, and how long has he been Yōu Lán?\n- **How did he leave the palace** — with his parents' blessing, against their wishes, by their quiet permission?\n- **Which of his siblings knows** — Jǐng, Wǔ, Lán? All, some, none?\n- **What does his face look like** as Yōu Lán vs as Chén Qīng — does he wear his Chén-family features openly at Hun Sect, or is there some change (hair, dress, eye-colour from Dream Walking's cultivation price) that renders him unrecognisable to visitors?\n- **The texture of his letters** to his mother — scroll or paper? Coded or plain? Carried by one of Hun Sect's spirit-birds or by human courier?\n- **When and how he first stepped into a dream** that showed him something he could not unsee.\n- **His bond with Yōu Lán's mist** — is it sanctuary or cell?\n- **The reveal moment** — when the Fellowship learns who the Dream Walker really is, and when Qīng allows himself to be seen.\n\n═══ HOW TO APPLY ═══\n\nWhen working with Qīng, lead with: the son who walked away from the imperial frame, the Dream Walker at Hun Sect, his mother's private channel into the sect world, his father's unvoiced suspicion, his brothers' quiet acknowledgement, his sister's possible shared knowing. He is the quietest of the four Chén children — and carries the densest intelligence picture outside the Crown Prince's own.\n\nWhen writing scenes set in Hun Sect, Yōu Lán is the figure who can be introduced without explanation first, his Chén identity revealed when the scene calls for it. The mist is the seam between the two names.
