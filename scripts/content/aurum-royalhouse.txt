AURUM — THE ROYAL HOUSE AND GUÌ THE HIGH CHANCELLOR\n\n═══ THE ONE-LINE TRUTH ═══\n\nThe Royal House of Aurum sits over the Hope-tree at the centre of the world's largest floating island, ruling a realm that has been quietly run for centuries by the same Shi Sect Blood Witch who fakes her death every fifty years and returns as the next High Chancellor. The throne does not know it has been conned for centuries. The Empress of Aoli does not know the same woman is wearing her murdered sister's face.\n\n═══ THE ROYAL FAMILY ═══\n\nThe Royal House of Aurum is the apex of the realm — Tier 6, the highest of the six tiers of altitude that organise Aurum society. They do not live on a static island; their seat is the GRANDEST SKY-VESSEL in the realm, anchored above the Royal Island for ceremonial occasions and free to drift the constellation of islands as state and weather demand.\n\nThe Royal Family is described in canon at the broad-strokes level — the sovereigns, their consorts, their children, the network of cousins and royal-house cadet branches. They wear pagoda-cut hanfu of imperial gold and crimson, ride airships with brass-and-jade fittings, hold court above the cloud layer where the air is thinnest and the light is most golden. They are the visual culmination of everything Aurum tells the world it is.\n\nThe Royal House is also the only tier of Aurum society that knows the FULL truth — they know about the blood magic; they know about the Underrock's role; they know the Dìwǔ tree is being drained; they know the original ancestors' magic has dried out. Each new monarch is taken into the secret by the previous one shortly before the succession. The truth is the throne's heaviest burden. **They keep it because they believe — wrongly — that the alternative is the realm falling from the sky.**\n\nWHAT THEY ARE NOT: villains. Cosmologically, the Royal House are NOT the saga's antagonists. They were CONNED, generations ago, by Guì the Blood Witch. They became cruel out of desperation, then complicit out of fear. By the time the current generation inherited the throne, the system had been running for centuries and stopping it without an alternative meant the realm falling — and they were never given an alternative. They have done the wrong thing for the right reasons for so long that the wrong has become the throne's posture.\n\n**The saga's structural truth about them:** the Royal House could become Aria's ALLIES if she can prove what Guì has done. They are not Yuèlì-aligned. They are exhausted, complicit, and waiting — even if they cannot say so — for someone to show them another way.\n\n═══ THE ROYAL ISLAND ═══\n\n**Serina's verbatim:** *\"The Diwu tree at the time that the islands were raised had been planted at the centre of this subterrianian lake. This portion of the lake has now become the home of the royal family.\"*\n\nThe Royal Island is the LARGEST of all the floating islands. It was given to the Royal Family because of the Dìwǔ tree at its centre — the throne over the Hope-tree, in the most literal sense. The original subterranean lake's central portion came up with the Dìwǔ when the ancestors lifted the land, and that portion of the lake is now the heart of the Royal Island.\n\nAround the centre of the island, where the lake-water glows beneath, the **Aurum Archival** — a massive library — was constructed centuries ago to HIDE the tree and preserve it. The tree stands in the Inner Sanctum of the Archival; the library was built around it. (See `aurum-culture` for the Archival; `aurum-minhui` for the silent caretaker who works there.) The Royal Palace is constructed alongside the Archival on the upper tiers of the same island, the throne and the tree linked spatially the way they are linked magically.\n\n**THE NOBLE-SHIP DOCKING.** When court is in session, the upper noble flying ship-homes DOCK at the Royal Island — tethered above and around the great vessel of the Royal House, their gangways reaching out to the island. This temporarily makes the Royal Island even larger; the central island swells into a swollen many-shipped capital with mobile palaces tethered in a constellation around it. Seen from a distance, the Royal Island in court session looks like a flower in bloom, petals of noble vessels opening around its centre.\n\nWhen court adjourns, the noble ships scatter to their own preferred altitudes, and the Royal Island returns to its bare grandeur — vast, mostly empty in its upper tiers, the great vessel of the Royal House silent above the silent Archival above the silent tree above the silent water.\n\n═══ GUÌ THE BLOOD WITCH — HIGH CHANCELLOR OF AURUM ═══\n\n**Serina's verbatim:** *\"The Shi sect saw and opening and sent Gui the Blood Witch to the royal family she was given instructions to infiltrate the royal house and strongly encourge the use of blood magic to keep the islands afloat. She became the High Chancollar and has had the postion ever since.\"*\n\nGuì is the centuries-old originator of the Shi Sect — the LIVING REMNANT of Yuèlì's pre-fall hands, one of the inner-circle fragment-carriers, a being far older than the Royal House she advises. The Shi Sect predates Yuèlì's fall — Yuèlì collected aligned servants BEFORE her madness fully descended and made them her network before her own children locked her up. **Every Shi Sect inner-circle member is centuries old.** Guì is one of them.\n\nWhen the original magic faded and Aurum's islands began to descend, the Shi Sect saw an opening. They sent Guì. She came in the robes of a learned outsider — a quiet, cultivated woman from across the sea, a scholar of old magics who had heard of Aurum's troubles and offered her counsel. She proposed the unthinkable: a small, ritualised use of life-force, channelled through the Dìwǔ tree, to arrest the descent. The Royal House — terrified, desperate, isolated from any other source of advice — agreed.\n\nThe technique worked. The descent slowed. Guì was rewarded with the position of **HIGH CHANCELLOR** — chief advisor to the throne, manager of the rituals she had introduced, keeper of the secret she had brought into the room.\n\nShe has held that position EVER SINCE.\n\n═══ THE 50-YEAR FAKERY ═══\n\n**Serina's verbatim:** *\"Every 50 years since she first assumed the role centuries before she has facked her death and come back as the newly appointed High Chancilor sent by the Shi Sect.\"*\n\nThe Royal House does not know Guì has been Chancellor for centuries. They believe each Chancellor is a fresh appointment by the Shi Sect — a new outsider scholar, sent every fifty years to continue the partnership. This is the lie Guì has maintained for as long as the system has been running.\n\n**The mechanism:**\n1. Guì serves as Chancellor for approximately fifty years — long enough to be valuable, short enough to be plausibly mortal.\n2. Near the end of her tenure, she falls into a public illness, or has a public accident, or simply ages visibly into a decline. The court mourns. A funeral is held. A body is buried (or, in some iterations, cremated or interred in a sealed sky-chapel where no one will ever look).\n3. The Royal House writes to the Shi Sect requesting a replacement. Within months, a new Chancellor arrives — a different woman, a different name, a different age, a different background, but always (always) bringing the same expertise.\n4. The new Chancellor is *the same woman* — Guì, with a different face, a different identity, a different cover story.\n5. She serves another fifty years. The cycle repeats.\n\n**How she manages the face changes** is open canon — Wen's read suggests blood-magic disguise (her own discipline lets her hold a different appearance for decades), or a slow physical reshaping over the death-and-return cycle, or possibly assistance from the wider Shi Sect network of fragment-carriers. What is locked is that the change is convincing enough that no Royal House across the centuries has ever publicly questioned it.\n\n**What the throne sees:** a useful succession of outside experts, each polite, each effective, each respectfully limited in tenure. **What is actually happening:** one woman, one chair, one continuous reign, dressed up as a series of replacements. *Same woman. Same chair. Costume of replacement.*\n\nGuì has been the High Chancellor of Aurum across more royal generations than any living royal could count. She has watched the Royal House inherit the secret she introduced. She has helped each new monarch make peace with the system she designed. She has refined the blood-magic apparatus across centuries. **Every refinement that makes Aurum more dependent on the Shi Sect technique is HER refinement.**\n\nShe is, functionally, the longest-serving ruler in Aurum's history — and nobody on the throne knows it.\n\n═══ THE STOLEN IDENTITY TACTIC — GUÌ = GUÌ XĪ = LÍ WǍN'S MURDERED SISTER'S STOLEN FACE ═══\n\nThis is where Aurum's saga arc binds into Aoli's, and it is one of the saga's darkest reveals.\n\n**Serina's verbatim:** *\"they are the same Lan's actual sister did fall inlove with Ming Jue and did follow him to the shi sect but she was killed her blood used and Gui Xi the blood witch took her place this way the shi sect would have and identity they can use to infiltrate the imperial family.\"*\n\nThe locked architecture:\n- **Guì Xī (the real one)** — Lí Wǎn's biological sister. She fell in love with Ming Jué (the corrupted heir of the Mù-aligned Aoli court network). She followed him to the Shi Sect. **She was MURDERED. Her blood was used by the Shi Sect.** Her identity was stolen.\n- **Guì the Blood Witch** — the centuries-old Shi Sect originator, the High Chancellor of Aurum — **TOOK THE DEAD SISTER'S PLACE.** She wears the dead sister's name and identity. The stolen identity gives the Shi Sect a way INTO the imperial family of Aoli through the Empress's most personal grief.\n- **Same witch. Two stolen-identity infiltrations.** TWO of the empire's greatest courts are compromised by the SAME WOMAN — Aurum's High Chancellor *and* Aoli's secret consort to the corrupted heir. One being. Two thrones.\n\n═══ THE TWO-COURT INFILTRATION ═══\n\nThe scope of what Guì has accomplished is staggering. She is simultaneously:\n\n**In Aurum:** The High Chancellor. Chief advisor to the Royal House. Architect of the blood-magic system that keeps the realm aloft. Resident of the Royal Island. Centuries of accumulated influence, every generation of royals brought to depend on her counsel. The realm runs because she designed it to.\n\n**In Aoli:** Guì Xī. The Blood Witch consort of Ming Jué. The Empress Lí Wǎn's \"sister.\" A face Lí Wǎn has been politely meeting at family functions for years. A name in the imperial family ledgers. A presence in the Mù-vs-Lí court war that is shaping the next generation of Aoli leadership.\n\nHow she physically maintains both identities is open canon. Possibilities:\n- Travel between courts (the Royal Island is far from the Aoli capital but a sky-vessel route is plausible)\n- Blood-magic distribution — like the Shi inner-circle fragment-carriers, she may be able to project presence into multiple bodies or hold an apprentice in one identity while she occupies the other\n- An apprentice playing one role while she plays the other, with periodic identity-swaps\n\nWhat is locked is that BOTH identities are her. Both courts are compromised. Both reigns are real.\n\n═══ IMPLICATIONS FOR LÍ WǍN — THE EMPRESS'S GRIEF MADE BOTTOMLESS ═══\n\nThis canon turns an already-dark thread of Lí Wǎn's life into something cosmic-horror grade.\n\n**Lí Wǎn has been politely meeting her sister's killer at family functions for years.** Whether she KNOWS or only SUSPECTS is an open question. But every \"Guì Xī\" interaction, every shared meal, every formal courtesy at imperial gatherings — it has all been with the witch who killed her real sister and stole her face.\n\nThe specific weight of this grief:\n- Lí Wǎn's sister fell in love with Ming Jué. Lí Wǎn watched her sister go. Then her sister came back as a Blood Witch consort — a different woman, in her sister's face.\n- The version of Lí Wǎn's sister Lí Wǎn has been meeting for years was the WITCH WHO MURDERED HER. The kindness she showed \"her sister\" has been showered on the killer.\n- Every formal title, every courtesy, every shared garden walk, every memory of their childhood that Lí Wǎn might have referenced in conversation — she has been generously giving the murderer a continuous performance of being beloved.\n- **Her grief is bottomless because the grief itself has been weaponised.** She has been sustaining the murderer's cover.\n\n**When she finds out** — and the saga will eventually require her to find out — Lí Wǎn becomes one of the few people who can UNMASK Guì. The woman who can call the witch by her stolen name and prove the murder. The Empress's grief, then her rage, becomes one of the saga's load-bearing forces.\n\n**Lí Wǎn's whole early-life suffering may be Shi-orchestrated.** The Mù-poisoning of Chén Míng (her first betrothed) and the loss of her sister may be braided together by Shi influence — a young woman robbed of two loves at once because the Shi Sect needed her family destabilised. If this binding is correct, the Empress of Aoli has spent her entire adult life being quietly farmed by the same network now ruling Aurum from the shadows.\n\n═══ IMPLICATIONS FOR ARIA'S SAGA ARC — THE THRONE-ROOM CONFRONTATION ═══\n\nAria's eventual return to Aurum as the prophecy child means walking into the throne room of a Royal House that has been quietly conned for centuries — and naming the truth they have not been able to see.\n\n**The structural beats of the throne-room confrontation:**\n\n1. **The Royal House is not the antagonist.** When Aria reaches the throne, she is not facing villains. She is facing centuries of inherited fear. Her job is not to destroy them — it is to give them what they have been silently waiting for: a way OUT of the system Guì designed for them.\n\n2. **Guì IS the antagonist.** When Aria confronts the High Chancellor, she is not just fighting one Blood Witch — she is fighting the centuries-old Shi Sect originator who has worn this identity longer than the saga's living kingdoms have existed. **Guì is Aria's direct antagonist when she returns home.**\n\n3. **The reveal is double.** Aria does not just expose what the Chancellor has been doing in Aurum — she exposes the SAME WOMAN'S Aoli identity. The two-court infiltration becomes visible in one moment. The Royal House sees the truth of their long-trusted Chancellor; the Empress of Aoli (likely informed by the same disclosure) sees the woman wearing her sister's face.\n\n4. **Lí Wǎn becomes a key witness.** If the saga arranges it, Lí Wǎn arrives in Aurum or sends a sealed testimony — the Empress of Aoli formally identifies the Aurum High Chancellor as the witch who murdered her sister and stole her face. *Two empires' grievances against one woman, named in one room.*\n\n5. **The Royal House faces the choice.** Confronted with the truth, they can side with Aria (and accept the realm's new path through Hope-tree healing rather than blood magic) or they can side with Guì (and become true villains by choice rather than accident). The saga gives them the choice their ancestors were denied.\n\n6. **The Hope-tree's healing is the system's answer.** When Aria's blood meets Fèngzhé's fragment in the Dìwǔ, the tree begins to remember its original covenant. The blood-magic dependence can end if the willing-offering covenant returns. The Royal House can save the realm without the Underrock dying for it. **This is the path Guì has spent centuries making sure they could not see.**\n\nThe throne-room confrontation is one of the saga's biggest scenes. The whole prophecy bends toward it.
