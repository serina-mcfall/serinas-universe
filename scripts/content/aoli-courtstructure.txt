THE AOLI IMPERIAL COURT STRUCTURE\nThe full court of the Chén Dynasty. Zhou-coded in bones, layered with later overlays. Three Ducal Ministers at the apex, Nine Ministers running the state, supporting offices and censorate below. The central political tension of the saga lives in this hierarchy — numbers vs levers, the Mù faction's stranglehold on legitimacy against the coalition of Lí, Lǔ, Cui, Chén, and neutrals.\n\n═══ THE IMPERIAL FAMILY ═══\n\n- **Emperor (皇帝 Huángdì)** — Chén Yuán, Son of Heaven, reigning sovereign.\n- **Empress (皇后 Huánghòu)** — Lí Wǎn.\n- **Dowager Empress (皇太后 Huáng Tàihòu)** — Mù Tàihòu, the Emperor's mother.\n- **Crown Prince (太子 Tàizǐ)** — Chén Jǐng.\n- **Princes (亲王 Qīnwáng)** — Chén Wǔ, Chén Qīng (concealed as Yōu Lán of Hun Sect).\n- **Princess (公主 Gōngzhǔ)** — Chén Lán.\n\nSee `aoli-emperor`, `aoli-empress`, `aoli-crownprince`, `aoli-princewu`, `aoli-princeqing`, `aoli-princesslan` for the full interior canon on each.\n\n═══ THE THREE DUCAL MINISTERS — 三公 SĀN GŌNG ═══\n\nThe apex tier of the court. Three great offices that counsel the Emperor, weigh against each other, and set the tone of the reign.\n\n**PRIME MINISTER / CHANCELLOR — 宰相 Zǎixiàng.** Head of government, oversees all ministries. **Held by the Mù patriarch — grandfather to all four Mù Jade Sisters.** The most powerful civil official at court. The current power head of the Mù conspiracy. The man the Empress Dowager's secret campaign is aimed at. He cannot be moved against directly without starting a war inside the Mù clan itself.\n\n**GRAND TUTOR — 太师 Tài Shī.** Senior advisor to the Emperor, ritual and wisdom keeper. **Held by Lí Wǎn's father, head of House Lí.** Brings the Lí house into direct contact with the throne's ritual and policy voice. The steady Lí counterweight to the Mù PM at the apex tier.\n\n**GRAND MENTOR — 太傅 Tài Fù.** The Crown Prince's tutor, ethical and scholarly counsel. **Held by Cuī Yuǎnjǐng's youngest paternal uncle** — a Cuī, brother of Yuǎnjǐng's father — and the saga's silent architect of justice for the murdered Chén Míng.\n\nThe Cui Uncle was **Chén Míng's best friend and study companion (伴读)**. They grew up together, were brothers in everything but blood. After Míng's murder by the Mù, Cui Uncle spent decades quietly doing five things at once: (1) arranging his own brother's marriage into the deposed empress's daughter to secure the imperial line Míng would have continued; (2) becoming Grand Mentor to Míng's nephew Jǐng to position himself at the future emperor's elbow; (3) feeding young Jǐng the first hints of his uncle's true death; (4) keeping Yuǎnjǐng safe in Wen Sect; and (5) WAITING — for Jǐng to be ready, the Mù to overreach, and Yuǎnjǐng to return to the court her grandmother was driven from. He is the silent third chair at the matriarch decision scene — Mù Tàihòu and Lí Wǎn's natural ally, the only man who has carried both halves of the secret (Míng's murder + the deposed empress bloodline) since before either matriarch had full access. Jǐng loves and trusts him profoundly with no idea what he has been carrying. The reveal will be one of the most emotional moments in Jǐng's arc.\n\nOpen canon: did Cui Uncle WITNESS Míng's murder, hear from a runner, find the body, or only prove the Mù did it years later? Determines vengeance-born-of-guilt vs grief vs cold proof.\n\n═══ THE NINE MINISTERS — 九卿 JIǓ QĪNG ═══\n\nThe working heads of state. The Mù brothers hold 4 of the 9 — the **LEGITIMACY STRANGLEHOLD** combination. The other five are held by the Lǔ, neutrals, and Lí-coalition figures.\n\n**1. LAW (刑部 Xíng Bù) — held by the ELDEST Mù brother (Yáohuá's father).** Face of the judicial corruption Méi is investigating. Most senior Mù brother. Of all of them, he has the most to lose if exposed.\n\n**2. PERSONNEL (吏部 Lì Bù) — held by the SECOND Mù brother (Yáozhēn's father).** Controls civil-service appointments. The Strategist daughter has a strategist father — and likely insider knowledge of every Mù-placed official in the empire.\n\n**3. REVENUE (户部 Hù Bù) — held by the THIRD Mù brother (Yáojiāo's father).** The \"indulgent father\" — controls the empire's money. Yáojiāo's cruelty is partly the cruelty of the well-funded.\n\n**4. RITES (礼部 Lǐ Bù) — held by the FOURTH (youngest) Mù brother (Yáosù's father).** The man who decides what is proper has an illegitimate daughter he refuses to acknowledge. The household hypocrisy is the saga's quietest, deepest indictment of the Mù.\n\n**5. WAR (兵部 Bīng Bù) — held by Lǔ Méi's father, a senior Lǔ general.** The highest non-Mù ministerial seat in the empire. **Méi's investigation is politically explosive precisely because of this** — the Mù faction cannot easily retaliate against the Minister of War's daughter. Her father's office has the weight to intervene when she goes too deep. When Jǐng elevates Méi as third consort, he elevates the Minister of War's daughter — political legitimacy is already there.\n\n**6. WORKS (工部 Gōng Bù) — held by a neutral scholar-engineer, possibly a Tián clan figure.** Low political stakes. Neutral space in the court map.\n\n**7. IMPERIAL HOUSEHOLD / CLAN AFFAIRS (宗正 Zōng Zhèng) — held by a senior Chén great-uncle of the Emperor, quiet ally to the matriarchs.** He was alive when the deposed empress was deposed. He protected her archival records in the clan archives when the Mù tried to scrub them. He and the Cui Uncle form a two-man silent network of truth-keepers — possibly known to each other, possibly not — each guarding one half of the secret. Cui: the living bloodline. This uncle: the documentary proof. When the matriarchs tell Jǐng about Yuǎnjǐng, the PROOF of her bloodline exists BECAUSE of this uncle's decades of quiet archive-protection. The second pillar at the matriarch decision scene.\n\n**8. COURT CEREMONIAL (太常 Tài Cháng) — held by a neutral scholar-priest, possibly a former cultivator.** Quiet bridge between court and sect cultures.\n\n**9. FOREIGN AFFAIRS / VASSALS (大鸿胪 Dà Hónglú) — held by a Lí-aligned diplomat who served in Maramir and Solis in his youth.** Plot-load-bearing for the Maramir investigation. He has personal contacts abroad from his youthful postings. When Lìyǐn's Maramir extension request is routed through Foreign Affairs, this man recognises what is being asked and provides official cover without ever being asked aloud. Knowing, active ally. Jǐng's network now has a courtly diplomat who makes the Maramir investigation structurally possible.\n\n═══ SUPPORTING OFFICES ═══\n\n**IMPERIAL GUARD (御林军 Yùlín Jūn).** Defends the Emperor, the capital, the palace. **Head: Chén Wǔ, the Second Prince.** The throne's living wall. Jǐng's brother and mother's warrior.\n\n**CAPITAL SECURITY OFFICE (京兆府 Jīngzhào Fǔ).** Capital criminal investigation, public order. Reports to the Ministry of Law (Mù). **Head: a corrupt non-Mù official elevated by the Mù** — a replaceable scapegoat for when Méi exposes him. Gives Méi a specific face to confront. The Mù can throw him to the wolves to protect themselves when the exposure comes. The site of Jǐng's year-old undercover investigation. The site of the broom incident.\n\n**CENSORATE (御史台 Yùshǐ Tái).** Anti-corruption watchdog; can impeach officials. **Head: a senior Lí cousin** — Lí-aligned but NOT Lí Wǎn's direct sibling. The Lí-cousin framing gives him Lí-loyal credentials without the partisan \"Empress's brother\" label — aligned enough to act in Lí interest, distant enough to claim independence. **This is the institutional ally Méi will find when her Mù-judicial-corruption investigation goes too deep.** Her private truth becomes institutional opposition through the Censorate. The Mù faction now has its first real structural opposition.\n\n**HANLIN ACADEMY (翰林院 Hànlín Yuàn).** Imperial scholars, edict-drafters, royal tutors. **Head: a senior Lí-aligned scholar.** Cultural weight, another Lí foothold in the court's quiet infrastructure.\n\n**INNER PALACE SERVICE (内务府 Nèiwù Fǔ).** Inner palace logistics. Thinned by the monogamous Emperor reducing this office's traditional weight. Not structurally needed on the scale a harem-based court would demand.\n\n═══ THE FACTION BALANCE — NUMBERS VS LEVERS ═══\n\nThe central political tension of the saga sits in this scoreboard.\n\n**THE MÙ FACTION (numerically smaller, but holds the legitimacy infrastructure):**\n- Prime Minister — the Mù grandfather — top of court\n- Four of nine Ministries: Law, Personnel, Revenue, Rites\n- Dowager Empress (Mù Tàihòu) — inner court matriarch\n- Half the Crown Princess Selection candidates — four Mù Jade Sisters in the consort pool\n\n**THE LÍ + LǓ + CUI + CHÉN + NEUTRAL COALITION (numerically larger, now deeply locked):**\n- Empress Lí Wǎn — inner court\n- Grand Tutor — Lí Wǎn's father, head of House Lí\n- Grand Mentor — Cui Uncle, silent justice-architect, keeper of Yuǎnjǐng's bloodline\n- Ministry of War — Lǔ Méi's father, highest non-Mù seat\n- Ministry of Foreign Affairs — Lí-aligned diplomat with Maramir/Solis contacts\n- Ministry of Imperial Household — Chén great-uncle, archive-protector\n- Ministry of Works — neutral scholar\n- Ministry of Court Ceremonial — neutral scholar-priest\n- Censorate head — senior Lí cousin\n- Imperial Guard — Chén Wǔ, throne-loyal\n- Hanlin Academy — senior Lí-aligned scholar\n\n**SCORE:** Mù 5 seats. Lí + Lǔ + Cui + Chén + Neutrals 7+ seats. **The Mù are outnumbered numerically but hold LEGITIMACY (Law, Personnel, Revenue, Rites) which is the deeper power.** That is the central political tension of the saga: numbers vs levers.\n\n═══ THE SILENT TRUTH-NETWORK ═══\n\nWithin the coalition, four figures each hold one piece of the Yuǎnjǐng-bloodline secret that the matriarch decision scene will finally surface.\n\n- **Cui Uncle (Grand Mentor)** — the living bloodline. He has kept Yuǎnjǐng safe in Wen Sect and shepherded Jǐng toward readiness.\n- **Chén great-uncle (Imperial Household)** — the documentary proof. He protected the deposed empress's archival records when the Mù tried to scrub them.\n- **Mù Tàihòu (Dowager Empress)** — the pact partner. The placer. The one moving Yuǎnjǐng as her blade.\n- **Lí Wǎn (Empress)** — the suspector. She has fragments from old palace rumours, Lí family records, conversations Mù Tàihòu has hinted at without confirming. No proof yet — until Yuǎnjǐng walks into court wearing the pendant.\n\nWhen the matriarch decision scene finally happens, these are the four who will be in the room (literally or by reference). The decades of quiet preservation culminate here.\n\n═══ NAMES — DEFERRED ═══\n\nPer Serina: *\"wait till scene needs the names then we can look at names.\"* The four Mù brothers and several officeholders are currently holding POSITIONAL names (e.g., \"Eldest Mù brother / Yáohuá's father,\" \"Lí Wǎn's father,\" \"the Grand Mentor sage\") until a scene needs them. Méi is investigating up the spine of the most powerful clan in the empire.\n- The Lí + Lǔ + Cui + Chén + Neutral coalition has MORE SEATS but the Mù hold LEGITIMACY levers (Law, Personnel, Revenue, Rites). The saga turns on whether the coalition can convert numbers into leverage.
