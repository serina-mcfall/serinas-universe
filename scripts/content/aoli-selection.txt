THE CROWN PRINCESS SELECTION — 选秀 XUǍNXIÙ\nThe ceremonial process by which Crown Prince Chén Jǐng takes his Crown Princess and his Mù-blood consort. Eight candidates: four noble general-clan daughters in the Crown Princess pool, four Mù Jade Sisters in the consort pool. Set within Aoli's Zhou-coded imperial system, run jointly by Empress Lí Wǎn and Dowager Empress Mù Tàihòu. Multiple rounds — appearance, lineage, talents, conversation, deportment. Jǐng steers outcomes by preference signalled at the right moments. Two reveals walk quietly into the hall: the granddaughter of the deposed empress wearing the imperial crest at her throat, and the illegitimate maid-cousin the Mù elders never planned to elevate.\n\n═══ CONCEPT + APPROACH ═══\n\nJǐng's strategy is already canon. Crown Princess from the four general-clan daughters — for military weight, peace, future security. Consort from the four Mù Jade Sisters — specifically a low-rank or illegitimate Mù girl, grateful, NOT the cousin the Mù elders are pushing. Plus a third consort off-Selection — Lǔ Méi, the pattern-seer investigator, LOVE in his three-wife trinity.\n\nThe ceremony is the SETTING in which Jǐng's strategy plays out. The matriarchs run the rounds; the factions push their candidates; Jǐng quietly steers the outcome through preference signalled at the right moments. The tension is that everyone thinks they are choosing — the Mù elders think they have pre-chosen Yáohuá as Crown Princess; the Lí push their generals; the Emperor watches — but Jǐng is running his own board beneath the visible one.\n\nEach candidate is a character in her own right, carrying a different wound, a different want, a different register. The ceremony forces eight of them into one hall and asks a question no one has agreed to: *who is the Crown Princess of Aoli going to be.*\n\n═══ THE FOUR GENERALS — THE CROWN PRINCESS POOL ═══\n\n━━━ XIÀ RUÒLÁN (夏若兰) — \"LIKE AN ORCHID\" ━━━\n\n**House Xià — the Northern General's clan.** Junior General of the Xià Army. Eighteen years old. Has been in the army since she was eleven — seven years of service before the Selection. Her father defends the gate leading into Àn Shān.\n\n**Personality coded:** He Yan from *Legend of the Female General*. Smart, clever, fierce, emotionally tested, capable. The female lead who disguised herself as her brother and lived as a man at the military academy.\n\n**Visual composite:** Zhou Ye + Zoey Meng + Bao Shang En. Classical Han beauty, sharp-strong-serious. Built for command and battle.\n\n**Skills and signatures.** **FACE-READING** — she can read microexpressions, intentions, emotion from a person's face. Strategist-to-strategist understanding with Jǐng immediately. She might see THROUGH his granite. **TWIN SWORDS** — dual-wielding, fast, dramatic, expert. A duelist, not a noble lady who knows fencing. **A WHIP** at her side — additional weapon. Versatile (disarm, pull, distance) and a symbol of authority.\n\n**Beast companion: EMBER the fox.** A single fox raised from a kit, loyal, travels everywhere with her. Coloured red, auburn, and black-red — *\"looks like it was born in fire.\"* Phoenix-coded by colour, fire-named.\n\n**The wound.** Her younger brother Hányǔ is bound to a wheelchair because of an accident she unintentionally caused when they were young. He can no longer walk, and more than likely never will. The guilt is total. Intentionality is irrelevant; she did it. This is almost certainly why she joined the army at eleven — to atone, to escape, to become strong enough to never harm someone she loves again. Her face-reading may have come from the same place — she has spent her childhood reading her brother's expressions because she could not bear to make him suffer in any way she did not catch first.\n\n**The orchid name.** Ruòlán = 若兰 = \"like an orchid.\" Jǐng's Crown Princess candidate carries his family's grief-flower in her name without knowing what the flower means in this family. When Lí Wǎn hears it she inhales. When Chén Yuán hears it he closes his eyes for a moment. Princess Chén Lán's name (orchid) and Xià Ruòlán's name (like an orchid) bind across generations — and the bind would mean nothing to Ruo herself.\n\n**Why she is here.** **CHOSEN BY HER FATHER. Attends out of filial respect. Thinks the Selection is a waste of her time. NOT TRYING TO WIN.** She is the structural gold — the Crown Princess candidate who is not playing the game is exactly the Crown Princess Jǐng's strategy needs. The Mù faction will underestimate her completely. Lí Wǎn will notice her precisely because she is not trying. Jǐng will see her and recognise his own posture — he has spent his entire life pretending to want the throne he does not fully want. This is the He Yan paradox in full — the woman who didn't want the position becomes the one most worthy of it.\n\n**First meeting Jǐng:** AT the Selection. They have never met before.\n\n━━━ TIÁN YÌ (田弈) — \"THE GO GAME\" ━━━\n\n**House Tián — the Eastern General's clan.** Seventeen years old. Scholar more than warrior. Her father holds the east.\n\n**Personality coded:** Chu Qiao — both the Princess Agents 楚乔传 version and Huangyang Tian Tian's rendering in *Rebirth* as one unified archetype. Warrior-strategist who rose through obstacles; the Chu Qiao inner fire applied to a scholar's form.\n\n**Visual composite:** Huangyang Tian Tian + Dilraba Dilmurat + Deng Enxi + Gulnazar. Exotic / steppe-coded / Uyghur-sculpted beauty — large striking eyes, distinct features. House Tián likely drawn from a frontier region where Aoli's Han-coded heartland has intermarried with steppe, nomadic, or mountain peoples over generations.\n\n**Her name IS the game.** 弈 yì = to play Go / the strategic game. She was solving a particularly difficult Go move when she first met Jǐng as a girl of fourteen — didn't even register him. She was busy.\n\n**Type — SCHOLAR more than warrior.** Strategic. Plans everything. Polar opposite of Ruo's reluctant warrior. Likes quiet places, often found with her nose buried in a book. **TALKS TO HERSELF** — signature distinctive detail. When working through a problem, the thinking comes out of her mouth in a low murmur. The court will find this strange. Jǐng will recognise it instantly as a strategist's tic.\n\n**HIDDEN AGENDA.** She is at the Selection FOR A REASON. There is something she is looking for. She is the candidate WHO IS PLAYING THE GAME for her own purpose — not the throne itself.\n\n**Family.** Older brother she does not get on with. Younger sister she finds spoiled and irritating in the extreme. Father is eastern general. Smaller family than Ruo, and notably no warmth from siblings.\n\n**Prior meeting with Jǐng.** At about fourteen, when Jǐng visited the eastern border, she was introduced to him. She barely registered him because she was busy with a Go problem. Asymmetric memory: she barely remembers him; he probably has very specific memory of her. A girl who didn't notice him would have stood out enormously for a Crown Prince used to being the centre of attention.\n\n**Relation to Ruo.** Ruo fights with steel; Yì fights with strategy. They are the two strongest candidates in the pool, and they will recognise each other instantly.\n\n━━━ LǓ TÍNG (鲁婷) — \"GRACEFUL\" ━━━\n\n**House Lǔ — the Southern General's clan.** Eighteen years old. The Lǔ clan is the ancient state of Lu, Confucius's homeland — weighty Confucian heritage.\n\n**Personality coded:** Zhuang Han Yan from *The Glory*. **CALCULATING, RUTHLESS, will use any means to get what she wants. She wants to BE Crown Princess.** Pure antagonist energy. The first candidate who openly, actively, ambitiously wants the throne.\n\n**Visual composite:** Chen Duling + Bai Lu + Zhao Lusi. Classical Han, soft-round-sweet-pivoting-hard. The opposite visual register to Ruo's battle-sharp beauty.\n\n**Wound architecture.** Mother died when she was young. Father took a second wife — the stepmother made her life horrible. (Same wound as Lǔ Méi's. Same mother. Opposite response.)\n\n**She wields her beauty.** First candidate for whom looks are deliberately strategic — Ruo doesn't think about her appearance, Yì doesn't care, Lǔ Tíng has built her entire life on knowing.\n\n**The family reaction map.** Every imperial figure reads her differently within seconds of her entry. Jǐng sizes her up immediately, KNOWS her deal, will watch to see if she can be useful. Lí Wǎn HATES her — strong word; the Empress does not waste hate. Chén Yuán thinks she is DANGEROUS. Wǔ is ON GUARD around her. Princess Lán is WITHHOLDING JUDGMENT. Ruo has no time for her. Yì ignores her entirely.\n\n**The threat is not winning.** Lǔ Tíng is NOT going to be Crown Princess — every candidate and every imperial figure sees through her in the first moment. But she is unlikely to leave the saga's stage cleanly. The threat she poses is what she does AFTER she loses — the candidate most likely to cause the Selection's first real crisis, to spread rumours, to sabotage, to scheme.\n\n**Her younger sister Lǔ Méi should have been the candidate.** Méi lost the family fight. The sisters have the same mother; they turned out opposites. Tíng hardened into the cruelty she received. Méi refused to.\n\n━━━ CUĪ YUǍNJǏNG (崔远景) — \"FAR VISTA\" ━━━\n\n**House Cuī — the Western Mountain-Pass General's clan.** The fourth and final cardinal border.\n\n**Personality coded:** Cuī Lín-coded + Jun Qi Luo (*Everlasting Love*) + Shěn Lí Gē (*Song of Glory*). Three women who lived in armor, comfortable in male spaces, refined beauty under battle dress. Quiet, composed, observant, dry-witted. Humor as a lifted eyebrow, deadpan one-word teases. Self-sufficient. Loyal but not submissive. Guarded softness underneath. Pragmatic about love. Homesick for the pass.\n\n**Visual:** Slender, athletic build (horseback + sword drill, not embroidery). Refined delicate features. Dark observant eyes, slow to blink. Long dark hair pulled back. Lightly sun-touched — frontier living, not court porcelain. Small scar, location TBD. **Habit of resting thumb on sword hilt even at rest.**\n\n**Daily dress.** Fitted armor or practical frontier wear — dark leathers, soft cottons beneath, cloak. Greens, slates, deep blues, muted crimson. Beautiful in formal silk but uncomfortable in it; tolerates ceremony like wet boots.\n\n**Weapons.** Always armed. Sword at her hip (with a name she tells no one). Bow within reach. Hidden short blade somewhere unexpected.\n\n**The bloodline — THE LOAD-BEARING REVEAL.** Yuǎnjǐng's GRANDMOTHER is a DEPOSED EMPRESS — **Xiè Yùnzhī (谢韫之)** — \"She Who Contains Hidden Treasure\" — title-stripped or set aside while still living by the Mù faction generations ago. Her Wen-sect hidden name is **Qiū Lán (秋兰) — \"Autumn Orchid.\"** The name Wen Sect calls her now. The name she chose when she went into hiding. Yuǎnjǐng's MOTHER is the deposed empress's daughter, a princess by birth, who married into the western military family and lives there now. **Yuǎnjǐng is a princess by rights** — but she does not claim it. Her family chose to step away from the imperial title. She is LOOSELY related to the imperial family by blood; DEEPLY related by political wound.\n\n**Her uncle — her mother's brother — died before Yuǎnjǐng was born.** Her mother tells her stories about him. Yuǎnjǐng has no memory of his face. He was the **ORIGINAL FIRST CROWN PRINCE** of the previous generation — engineered to death by the same Mù faction that later killed Chén Míng.\n\nThe multi-generational Mù pattern: the Mù faction has been systematically removing inconvenient crown princes from rival lines for generations. Yuǎnjǐng's uncle (the deposed empress's son), then Chén Míng (Chén Yuán's elder brother), and if no one stops them, perhaps Jǐng himself next.\n\n**The two-mode shaping.** Yuǎnjǐng is being trained as BOTH Crown Princess AND weapon. Court protocol, ceremonial deportment, languages, calligraphy, governance theory — the social machinery of an empress-to-be. Composure as armor, never look inward, hesitate-proof, observation skills — the silent blade. Mù Tàihòu's plan is for Yuǎnjǐng to WIN the Selection AND dismantle the Prime Minister. Her whole life trajectory has been engineered toward this dual destiny.\n\n**The age-10 meeting.** At ten she was brought before her grandmother by her mother. She was never told why. That meeting is when the decision was made — she would be shaped into both Crown Princess and weapon. The haunted memory she will eventually have to interrogate.\n\n**The grandmother hides in WEN SECT.** The deposed empress has been hiding there for decades. **Chén Xī and Zhēn Yǔ both know who she is and protect her identity fiercely.** Mù Tàihòu KNOWS and is the pact partner. Lí Wǎn SUSPECTS — until Yuǎnjǐng walks into court wearing the pendant, at which point near-certainty lands in a single look.\n\n**The jade pendant with the imperial royal crest.** The grandmother's gift to Yuǎnjǐng, given when she was ten. She has worn it on a cord around her neck for eight years. Daily. Always. She has never been told what the crest is. The saga's quietest ticking clock. Every Selection scene is a scene where a courtier could glance at her throat and recognise it. Mù Tàihòu placed her at the Selection KNOWING she wears it — a deliberate power move, sending the wronged line to court wearing the evidence of her bloodline as a quiet dare.\n\n**The wound — existential.** Her composure is TRAINED. Her self-sufficiency is GROOMED. She was shaped — by her grandmother through her mother, since childhood — to make her face unreadable, to not look inward, to be hesitate-proof. When she begins to piece this together, the crisis of her life will be: *Am I real? Or am I my grandmother's final lesson?* This makes her the most internally fragile of the four general candidates.\n\n**The Crown Prince is not the plan.** If she falls for Jǐng — or he for her — it BREAKS THE SCHEME. Yuǎnjǐng is the most dangerous \"real\" love-interest for Jǐng — because loving her means siding with her against the Empress Dowager's mission.\n\n═══ THE FOUR-ORCHID SIGNATURE ═══\n\nThe saga's flower-of-grief is a multi-generational symbolic pattern across three generations of the Aoli imperial line:\n\n- **Chén Yuán's father's reign:** Qiū Lán (deposed empress's hidden name) — grieving her son the original first crown prince.\n- **Chén Yuán's reign:** Chén Míng's orchids — the brother poisoned by the Mù clan.\n- **Chén Yuán's reign:** Princess Chén Lán — named for her uncle's flower of grief.\n- **The Selection:** Xià Ruòlán (\"like an orchid\") — unknowing candidate carrying the imperial wound in her name.\n\nEvery wronged or lost figure of the Aoli imperial line is named in orchids. None of them know about each other yet. When Yuǎnjǐng meets Qiū Lán on the Wen mountain and learns her grandmother's hidden name is Autumn Orchid, the four-orchid pattern locks into place.\n\n═══ THE FOUR MÙ JADE SISTERS — THE CONSORT POOL ═══\n\n**STRUCTURAL CANON.** The four Mù girls are COUSINS, ages 16-18, daughters of four Mù brothers, granddaughters of the Prime Minister. All four named with the generational character **瑶 (yáo, \"precious jade\")** — the Mù elders' deliberate signature of clan unity. The Jade Sisters as a coordinated faction offering. Each is a different facet of jade.\n\n**The Mù elders have PRE-CHOSEN.** The Prime Minister has told the family — quietly, before the Selection began — that **Yáohuá is the predetermined Crown Princess and the other three are window dressing.** Each cousin enters with an assigned role. Yáohuá believes it utterly. Yáozhēn quietly defies — the first crack in the Mù line. The other two, as yet unplayed.\n\n**The Mù faction's court lock.** The Prime Minister + four Mù brothers = the TOP FIVE CIVIL OFFICES of Aoli. Complete generational stranglehold on the government's bureaucratic spine. Now attempting to lock in the next generation through the Selection.\n\n━━━ MÙ YÁOHUÁ (穆瑶华) — \"JADE SPLENDOR\" — THE CHOSEN ━━━\n\n**Eldest cousin, 18.** The one the Mù elders have groomed for the throne her entire life. Daughter of the ELDEST Mù brother (Minister of Law).\n\n**Visual composite:** Zhu Xudan + Wang Yifei + Cheng Xiao. Tall, statuesque, dancer-built, cool classical ice beauty. Height + dancer grace + refined ice. She moves like someone who has been told her movements matter.\n\n**Personality — ARISTOCRATIC HAUTEUR.** Not cruel — DISTANT. Her contempt is in implication, not action. She does not sneer or insult or scheme. **She just looks past people.** She thinks she has already won — every adult she has known has told her, since childhood, that the Crown Princess seat is hers. She has internalised this completely. **She HATES IMPERFECTION** — both in herself and in others. Will be immaculate at all times. Will visibly disdain flaws in others. **Believes everyone has a social place and hers is at the top.** Not social-climbing — sincere, settled belief.\n\n**Jǐng reads her in three seconds and is mildly bored.** She will not understand his lack of interest. Her humiliation is not that she loses, it is that she discovers she was never the protagonist at all.\n\n━━━ MÙ YÁOZHĒN (穆瑶珍) — \"JADE TREASURE\" — THE STRATEGIST ━━━\n\n**Second cousin, 17.** Nickname **Zhēn'er** — affectionate diminutive, only used by people she has LET IN. The court knows her as the formal Mù Yáozhēn; only people she trusts (or who have earned her secret regard) get Zhēn'er. Daughter of the SECOND Mù brother (Minister of Personnel).\n\n**Personality coded:** Xue Fang Fei from *The Double* (墨雨云间). Strategic, observant, quietly vengeful, builds alliances patiently, strikes when the moment is right, warm-and-ruthless in the same body.\n\n**Visual composite:** Wu Jin Yan + Ai Mi + Yang Chaoyue. Soft, youthful, demure, UNDERESTIMATED.\n\n**The perfect predator in the perfect prey costume.** Quietly ambitious. Chooses to actually compete despite being told she shouldn't. *\"If she is going to be here she is going to be here and win.\"* Observes. Listens. Waits. Calculates moves. Go-mind disguised as deference. She KNOWS the Mù elders have written her off — competes anyway. **First quiet defection from the Mù elders' assigned hierarchy.** Wears demure as armor; her softness is strategic posture, not nature.\n\n**She and Tián Yì will recognise each other instantly.** Two real strategists in a room of performers. Possibly the saga's most surprising cross-faction friendship. Most likely candidate to SPOT THE PENDANT on Yuǎnjǐng's throat. Most dangerous candidate to Jǐng's plan, because she can READ it and play to it.\n\n━━━ MÙ YÁOJIĀO (穆瑶娇) — \"JADE PAMPERED\" — THE CRUEL ONE ━━━\n\n**Third cousin, 17.** Daughter of the THIRD Mù brother (Minister of Revenue). The indulgent father — bluster + status + the kind of father who gives his daughter everything she wants because he likes feeling powerful through her.\n\n**Personality coded:** Jian Rou Yao from *The Double*. Spoiled, vain, jealous, throws tantrums when denied, performatively sweet to those above, openly cruel to those below, plays victim when caught. Pure entitlement-as-action.\n\n**Visual composite:** Xia Meng + Zoey Meng + Liu Shi Shi. Classical refined sharp Han beauty.\n\n**SPOILED-CRUEL, not survivor-cruel.** No real grievance — she harms because she can. Distinct from Lǔ Tíng, who at least has a wound source. No depth. No redemption arc. She is vain, obsessed with her own beauty, jealous of others' attention, throws tantrums when denied, plays victim when caught. Uses her position to crush others — for fun, not advantage.\n\n**She resents Yáohuá** — feels SHE should have been the Chosen. Will undermine Yáohuá behind her back. Internal Mù-cousin tension the Mù elders did not anticipate. **She and Lǔ Tíng will recognise each other as fellow antagonists** — and almost certainly clash. Will be cruel to Yuǎnjǐng — frontier-coded plain girl, easy target — with no idea what Yuǎn carries at her throat. The dramatic irony will be excellent. **Most likely to cause the Selection's first real crisis** — sabotage, poisoning attempt, vicious public humiliation of a rival.\n\n━━━ MÙ YÁOSÙ (穆瑶素) — \"JADE PLAIN\" — THE GRATEFUL ONE ━━━\n\n**Fourth cousin, 16.** The youngest of the four cousins. The youngest of the eight candidates. **THE LINCHPIN OF JǏNG'S STRATEGY.**\n\n**The backstory.** The Prime Minister has 4 sons + 1 daughter. The daughter — the 5th Mù sibling — conceived a child out of wedlock (disgrace) and died in childbirth. The illegitimate baby was Yáosù. **Family custom:** illegitimate Mù children go to the eldest brother's household. **Yáosù was raised by Yáohuá's father.** In the same household as Yáohuá. House-sisters of opposite fates: prized chosen jewel + maid who never got to be a girl. Yáohuá has spent her entire life treating Yáosù like furniture.\n\n**Open canon tension.** The locked ministerial assignment says Yáosù's FATHER is the 4th Mù brother (Minister of Rites) — \"the man who decides what is proper has an illegitimate daughter he refuses to acknowledge.\" But she was raised by her uncle Yáohuá's father (eldest brother) per family custom. The two details need reconciling when a scene calls it out.\n\n**素 sù** — plain / simple / unadorned + white silk (cloth of mourning, cloth without rank). ALSO means essence / the original true substance — a hidden second meaning the family did not consider. The family literally named her PLAIN — wrote their contempt on her papers. **When Jǐng pronounces her name, the contempt dissolves.** In his mouth \"Yáosù\" reads as \"Jade TRUE.\" Same name, transfigured meaning.\n\n**Visual composite:** Zhang Ruonan + Tian Xiwei + Ren Min. Young, soft, natural, understated. The kind of beauty you don't notice until you really look — and then you can't look away. Possibly looks younger than 16 (closer to 14-15 visually).\n\n**Visible details of her servant status.** Hands rough from work. Hair simply done — no time to elaborate-braid. Plain robes, possibly hand-me-downs from her cousins. Sometimes visibly tired.\n\n**Personality.** Genuinely sweet — kindness is her nature, not posture. Quiet — the only quiet candidate among loud/cold/showy others. Has been taught her whole life to be INVISIBLE. Tries to disappear, fails — she thinks she succeeds; she does not. **Has never been loved** — clean devastating wound. Servant-coded posture, language, deference.\n\n**Why she fits Jǐng's strategy.** Raised in service → grateful, not entitled. No faction loyalty — the family treats her as maid; she owes them nothing. Intelligent — Jǐng sees this even though no one else does. Genuinely kind — rare in the pool. Brings the Mù connection his strategy needs WITHOUT the Mù faction's controlling influence. The Mù elders cannot publicly object — she IS technically a Mù. **The moment Jǐng signals interest in her, the entire Mù line fractures publicly. And the Prime Minister cannot say a word.**\n\n**Reaction map.** Ruo sees her and wants to PROTECT her (her brother-trauma protective instinct activates). Yuǎnjǐng sees her and thinks she needs to be TRAINED to protect herself (frontier-soldier response). Yì does not see her (nose in a book). **Jǐng SEES HER for who she is and what she brings.**\n\n**Mù Tàihòu is her dead niece's daughter — her own grand-niece by blood.** When Jǐng picks Yáosù, Mù Tàihòu may be quietly the most pleased person in the room. Two empresses (Qiū Lán the deposed + Mù Tàihòu the reigning) and the Crown Prince all rescuing the wronged girls in their respective lines, on the same day.\n\n═══ THE THIRD CONSORT — LǓ MÉI (OFF-SELECTION) ═══\n\n**Lǔ Tíng's gentler younger sister, Lǔ Méi, is added to Jǐng's marriage as a THIRD CONSORT.** She is NOT a Selection candidate — she arrives at court as her sister's family support, but Jǐng has loved her for a year since their broom-incident meet-cute at the Capital Security Office. **The third-consort move is OFF-SELECTION** — bypasses the formal ceremony.
